## What is PIE?

Proto-Indo-European (PIE) is the theorized common ancestor of most European and Indian languages. Spoken approximately 4500-2500 BCE, it is not attested in texts but reconstructed through comparative linguistics.

Understanding PIE roots reveals deep connections between seemingly unrelated words and unlocks the fundamental concepts encoded in language itself.

## Essential PIE Roots for WYRD

### *ǵneh₃-* (to know)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *jñā* (to know) → *jñāna* (knowledge, wisdom)
- Greek: *gnōsis* (knowledge) → gnostic, diagnosis
- Latin: *noscere* (to know) → notion, notice, narrative
- English: *know*, *ken* (awareness), *canny* (knowing)
- Slavic: *znati* (to know)

**WYRD Angle:** Knowledge is recognition—re-cognizing what was already there. Gyan Mudrā: the gesture of knowing.

---

### *dʰeh₁-* (to place, put)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *dhā* (to place) → *dhyāna* (meditation, placing attention)
- Greek: *thesis* (placing) → thesis, synthesis, theme
- Latin: *facere* (to do, make) → fact, factory, fashion
- English: *do*, *deed*
- German: *tun* (to do)

**WYRD Angle:** Doing, making, placing, meditating—all share a root. To act is to place attention deliberately.

---

### *kerd-* (heart)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *hṛd* (heart)
- Greek: *kardia* (heart) → cardiac
- Latin: *cor* (heart) → cordial, courage, concord
- English: *heart*
- German: *Herz*

**WYRD Angle:** Heart = core = courage. To be courageous is to act from the heart. Credit asks for heart-trust.

---

### *h₁est-* (to be)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *as* (to be)
- Greek: *einai* (to be) → entity, essence
- Latin: *esse* (to be) → essence, interest (inter-esse)
- English: *is*, *was*, *were*
- German: *ist*, *war*

**WYRD Angle:** The most fundamental concept—existence itself—shares a root across all Indo-European languages.

---

### *weyd-* (to see, know)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *vid* (to know) → *veda* (knowledge), *vidyā* (wisdom)
- Greek: *eidenai* (to know) → idea, ideologue
- Latin: *vidēre* (to see) → vision, video, evident
- English: *wit* (knowledge), *wise*, *vision*
- Slavic: *videti* (to see)

**WYRD Angle:** Seeing = knowing. Vision is not just physical sight but understanding. The wise are those who see clearly.

---

### *dheh₁-* (to do, put, place)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *dhā* (to place, support) → *dharma* (that which supports)
- Greek: *thesis* (placing) → thesis, antithesis
- Latin: *facere* (to make) → fact, faction, fashion
- English: *do*, *deed*, *doom* (judgment, what is set/placed)

**WYRD Angle:** Dharma = that which holds/places. Your essential nature is what supports you when everything else falls away.

---

### *men-* (to think)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *man* (to think) → *manas* (mind), *mantra* (thought-form)
- Greek: *menos* (mind, spirit) → mental, mnemonic
- Latin: *mens* (mind) → mental, mention, dementia
- English: *mind*, *memory*, *man* (human, thinker)

**WYRD Angle:** Man = thinker. The human is the one who thinks. Mantra = that which liberates through thinking.

---

### *bʰeh₂-* (to speak, say)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *bhāṣ* (to speak)
- Greek: *phēmi* (to say) → phone, phoneme
- Latin: *fārī* (to speak) → fate, infant (in-fans, not speaking)
- English: *ban* (proclamation), *boast*

**WYRD Angle:** Speaking creates fate. What you speak becomes. The Frequency Warrior speaks deliberately.

---

### *stā-* (to stand)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *sthā* (to stand) → *stha* (standing), *prasthāna* (starting point)
- Greek: *histēmi* (to stand) → static, system, ecstasy (ek-stasis, standing out)
- Latin: *stāre* (to stand) → state, status, stable
- English: *stand*, *stay*, *stead*

**WYRD Angle:** Understanding = standing under. To understand is to stand under the concept, to support it with your presence.

---

### *h₁er-* (to move, rise)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *ṛ* (to rise, move)
- Greek: *orinomai* (to rise)
- Latin: *orīrī* (to rise) → origin, orient, abort
- English: *rise*, *raise*

**WYRD Angle:** Orient = facing the rising (east). Origin = the rising point. The east is where the sun rises—where everything begins.

---

### *weǵ-* (to be active, awake)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *vāj* (strength, vigor)
- Latin: *vigil* (awake) → vigil, vigilant, vigor
- English: *wake*, *watch*, *waken*

**WYRD Angle:** Vigor = wakefulness. The Frequency Warrior is vigilant—awake to the game being played.

---

### *ser-* (to protect, preserve)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *pāla* (protector)
- Latin: *servāre* (to save, keep) → conserve, preserve, reserve
- English: *hearse* (originally: rake for clearing), *harvest* (gathering, preserving)

**WYRD Angle:** Conservation = protection. What are we protecting? From what? For whom?

---

### *lewk-* (light, brightness)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *roka* (light), *loka* (world, illuminated place)
- Greek: *leukos* (white, bright) → leukemia, leuka
- Latin: *lux* (light) → lucid, illuminate, lucifer (light-bringer)
- English: *light*, * leuk-* (white blood cells)

**WYRD Angle:** Loka = world = illuminated place. The world is what is lit up. Darkness is not absence but unlit presence.

---

### *ghosti-* (stranger, guest, host)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *ghoshti* (assembly, guests)
- Greek: *xenos* (stranger, guest, host) → xenophobia
- Latin: *hostis* (stranger, enemy) → hostile, host
- English: *guest*, *host*, *hospitality*, *hostile*

**WYRD Angle:** Guest and host share a root—both are positions in relationship to the stranger. The stranger can be welcomed (guest) or feared (hostile).

---

### *dō-* (to give)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *dā* (to give) → *dāna* (giving)
- Greek: *didōmi* (to give) → dose, donate
- Latin: *dare* (to give) → date (given), data (things given)
- English: *donate*, *dowry*, *endow*

**WYRD Angle:** Data = things given. Information is a gift. Be careful what you give and receive.

---

### *gʷerh₃-* (to devour, feed)
**Descendants:**
- Sanskrit: *gras* (to swallow)
- Greek: *bibrōskō* (to eat)
- Latin: *vorāre* (to devour) → voracious, carnivore, herbivore
- English: *feed*, *foster*, *food*

**WYRD Angle:** Carnivore = flesh-devourer. The word reveals the violence in the act.

## WYRD Connections

### The "Knowing" Cluster
- *ǵneh₃-* → know, gnosis, jñāna, wit
- *weyd-* → see, veda, vision, wise
- *men-* → mind, mental, manas, mantra

**Pattern:** Knowing is seeing is thinking. The roots intertwine—true knowledge requires all three.

### The "Placing" Cluster
- *dʰeh₁-* → place, do, make, thesis, dhyāna
- *stā-* → stand, stay, state, stha
- *ser-* → preserve, conserve, serve

**Pattern:** To exist is to be placed, to stand, to be preserved. Being is relational.

### The "Giving/Receiving" Cluster
- *dō-* → give, donate, data, dāna
- *ghosti-* → guest, host, hospitality

**Pattern:** Exchange creates relationship. Data (things given) connects us.

## Application in WYRD Practice

### Step 2 Enhancement
When tracing word roots, always check PIE:
1. Look up the word in etymonline.com
2. Note the PIE root if listed
3. Check cognates in related languages
4. Look for the fundamental concept

### Liberation Angle Deepening
PIE roots reveal:
- **What concepts are fundamental to humans** (they appear across all descendant languages)
- **What connections we've forgotten** (guest/host, heart/core/courage)
- **What assumptions are encoded** (knowing = seeing; being = standing)

### Example: "Truth" Revisited
- Old English: *triewth* (faithfulness)
- PIE: *dru-* (firm, solid, enduring)
- Sanskrit: *dru* (firm), *druh* (to be hostile—firm against)
- Greek: *dru-* (oak tree—firm, enduring)

**Liberation Angle:** Truth = firmness, endurance. Not agreement, not consensus. What stands firm. The oak is true to itself.
