## Cross-Cutting Themes

### 1. Activity vs. Passivity

Many words reveal that perception and temporal experience are **active processes**:
- **See** may mean "follow with the eyes"
- **Look** originally meant "spy" — intentional attention
- **Watch** derives from "be awake" — maintained consciousness
- **Time** means "to cut/divide" — we create time through measurement

**Implication:** We are not passive recipients of experience but active participants in constituting reality.

### 2. The Vital Dimension

Several roots connect to **life-force**:
- *weg-* (watch) = to be strong, lively
- *aiw-* (forever) = vital force, life, eternity
- *leuk-* (light) = brightness as life-giving

**Implication:** Perception, time, and eternity are not abstract categories but expressions of living vitality.

### 3. Gender and Cosmology

The celestial entries reveal **grammatical gender** encoding ancient cosmology:
- **Sun** was feminine (*sunne*) in Old English, became masculine
- **Moon** was masculine (*mona*) in Old English
- Both shifted toward modern stereotypes (sun/masculine, moon/feminine)

**Implication:** Language encodes cosmological assumptions that change over time.

### 4. The Measurement Theme

Several words derive from "measuring":
- **Moon** (*me-* = to measure) — the original timepiece
- **Time** (*deh₂y-* = to divide/cut) — measuring duration
- **World** (*wer* + *ald* = man's age) — the measured span of human life

**Implication:** Human experience of cosmos and time is fundamentally structured by measurement practices.
