## Power & Control

### Government
- **Old French:** *governer* → Latin *gubernare* (to steer, pilot a ship)
- **Greek:** *kybernan* (to steer)
- **PIE:** *geubh-* (to curve, bend, arch)
- **Liberation Angle:** The government is presented as "steering" society, but ships have destinations chosen by captains. Who chooses society's destination?

### Authority
- **Latin:** *auctoritas* (influence, command)
- **From:** *auctor* (originator, promoter)
- **Root:** *augere* (to increase, to grow)
- **Liberation Angle:** Authority originally meant "that which causes growth." Modern authority often stunts growth. Reclaim the root: true authority increases capacity, not control.

### Control
- **Old French:** *contreroller* (to check, verify against a register)
- **From:** *contra* (against) + *rotulus* (roll, register)
- **Literal:** To check against the roll
- **Liberation Angle:** Control began as verification—checking reality against a list. Now it means forcing reality to match the list. The Frequency Warrior checks lists against reality.

### Manage
- **Latin:** *manus* (hand)
- **From:** *maneggiare* (to handle, especially horses)
- **Literal:** To handle, as one handles an animal
- **Liberation Angle:** Management began as handling horses. The assumption remains: those managed are like horses—beasts of burden requiring direction.

### Rule
- **Latin:** *regula* (straight stick, pattern)
- **PIE:** *reg-* (to move in a straight line, to direct)
- **Related:** Regal, regulate, right (correct), right (direction)
- **Liberation Angle:** To rule is to straighten. But life is curved, cyclical, wild. The straight line is an abstraction imposed on organic reality.

## Economics & Value

### Money
- **Latin:** *moneta* (where coins were minted)
- **From:** *monēre* (to warn, to remind)
- **Literal:** The warning/remembering place
- **Liberation Angle:** Money was originally tied to memory and warning. It has become amnesia—making us forget what has real value.

### Debt
- **Latin:** *debitum* (thing owed)
- **From:** *debēre* (to owe, ought, must)
- **PIE:** *de-* (away from) + *habe-* (to have)
- **Literal:** To have away from you
- **Liberation Angle:** Debt is literally "not having." The modern economy runs on creating absence, then charging for presence.

### Interest
- **Latin:** *interesse* (to be between, to make a difference)
- **From:** *inter* (between) + *esse* (to be)
- **Literal:** That which exists between
- **Liberation Angle:** Interest originally meant "what lies between"—the difference, the gap. Now it means the gap widens forever, extracting from the space between.

### Credit
- **Latin:** *credere* (to believe, trust)
- **PIE:** *kerd-* (heart)
- **Literal:** Belief, trust, from the heart
- **Liberation Angle:** Credit asks for heart-trust in a system that has no heart. They want you to believe—belief without evidence is faith, and their god is profit.

### Value
- **Latin:** *valēre* (to be strong, be worth)
- **Related:** Valiant, valid, valor
- **Literal:** Strength
- **Liberation Angle:** Value = strength. What strengthens you? That has value. Everything else is distraction.

### Profit
- **Latin:** *proficere* (to make progress, be useful)
- **From:** *pro-* (forward) + *facere* (to make)
- **Literal:** To make forward
- **Liberation Angle:** Profit was progress. Now profit often requires regression—of workers, of environment, of soul. True profit moves all forward.

- **Germanic:** *triuwi-* (having fidelity)

### Fact
- **Latin:** *factum* (thing done, deed)
- **From:** *facere* (to do, make)
- **Literal:** That which has been made/done
- **Liberation Angle:** Facts are made. They are constructions, not revelations. Who made them? To what end?

## Spirituality & Religion

### Religion
- **Latin:** *religiō* (scrupulousness, moral obligation)
- **Possibly:** *re* (again) + *ligere* (to bind)
- **Or:** *religere* (to re-read, consider carefully)
- **Literal:** Re-binding; or careful consideration
- **Liberation Angle:** If religion is "re-binding," what are we binding ourselves to? The Frequency Warrior binds to direct experience, not institutions.

### Spirit
- **Latin:** *spiritus* (breath, breathing, wind)
- **From:** *spīrāre* (to breathe)
- **Literal:** Breath, wind, life force
- **Liberation Angle:** Spirit = breath. You don't have spirit; you are spirit (breathing). The word for spirit and breath is the same in many languages (Hebrew *ruach*, Sanskrit *prāṇa*). Spirit is not somewhere else. It is this breath.

### Holy
- **Old English:** *hālig* (holy, consecrated)
- **From:** *hāl* (whole, uninjured)
- **Literal:** Whole, complete, undivided
- **Liberation Angle:** Holy = whole. The sacred is not separate—it is the recognition of wholeness. Division is the profane.

### Sacred
- **Latin:** *sacer* (holy, consecrated)
- **PIE:** *sak-* (to sanctify)
- **Literal:** Set apart, dedicated
- **Liberation Angle:** Sacred = set apart. But what is set apart can be claimed. The truly sacred cannot be owned—only recognized.

### Bless
- **Old English:** *blēdsian* (to consecrate with blood)
- **From:** *blōd* (blood)
- **Literal:** To mark with blood
- **Liberation Angle:** To bless originally meant to mark with blood—claiming, sacrificing, dedicating. Modern "blessing" has lost the blood. But true blessing costs something.

### Pray
- **Old French:** *preier* → Latin *precāri* (to ask earnestly, beg)
- **From:** *prex* (prayer, request)
- **Literal:** To ask
- **Liberation Angle:** Prayer = asking. But asking whom? And for what? The Frequency Warrior asks themselves—and listens.

### Worship
- **Old English:** *weorthscipe* (worthiness, honor)
- **From:** *weorth* (worth) + *scipe* (state, condition)
- **Literal:** State of worth, acknowledgment of value
- **Liberation Angle:** Worship = acknowledging worth. What has worth? The Frequency Warrior worships by recognizing true value—not by submission.
