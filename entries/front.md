#### Front

**Word:** Front  
**Type:** Noun/Adjective (Position)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /frʌnt/

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** Foremost part; face; battle line; facade
- **Associations:** Face, leadership, facade, honesty, confrontation
- **Emotional response:** Confidence, exposure, confrontation, shame

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

| Stage | Form | Meaning | Notes |
|-------|------|---------|-------|
| **Modern English** | *front* | Face, foremost part, facade | Multiple senses |
| **Late 13c.** | *front* | Forehead, face, front | From French |
| **Old French** | *front* | Forehead, face, front | From Latin |
| **Latin** | *frons* / *frontem* | Forehead, brow, face, front | Original sense |
| **Latin** | *frons* | Forehead, countenance, expression | Extended |

**The Etymological Evidence:**

> "late 13c., 'forehead,' from Old French front 'forehead, brow' (12c.), from Latin frontem (nominative frons) 'forehead, brow, front; countenance, expression (especially as an indicator of truthfulness or shame); facade of a building, forepart,' a word of 'no plausible etymology' (de Vaan)." — *Online Etymology Dictionary*

*Front* derives from *frons* = forehead, brow — the front of the human head, the place of expression and exposure.

The Latin *frons* already carried moral weight: the forehead/brow was "especially as an indicator of truthfulness or shame." The front was where character showed — the place you could not hide.

**Critical insight:** Front is the forehead — the exposed, expressive, unhideable face. The front is not merely "forward" but *faced* — the direction of exposure, expression, encounter.

---

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

| Era | Meaning | Context |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **Latin** | *frons* = forehead, brow | Anatomical |
| **Late Latin** | Countenance, expression | Moral reading |
| **Late Latin** | Facade of building | Architectural |
| **Late Latin** | Front part of army | Military |
| **Late 13c.** | Face, forehead, front | Borrowed into English |
| **Mid-14c.** | Foremost part of anything | Generalization |
| **1895** | Appearance concealing truth | "Put up a front" |

**What Was Lost:**

The original *frons* as forehead — the place of shame and truthfulness — has been generalized into mere "forward position." We've lost the moral significance of the front: the face you show is the character you claim.

The "facade" sense (concealing true feelings) emerged by 1895 — an inversion of the original meaning. The *frons* that revealed truth became the *front* that conceals it.
