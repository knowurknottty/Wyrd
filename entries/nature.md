#### Nature

**Word:** Nature  
**Type:** Noun (Concept)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /ˈneɪtʃər/

**Step 1: Surface Reading**
- Common meaning: The physical world, plants and animals, natural forces
- Associations: Wilderness, purity, biology, the outdoors, what's "natural"
- Emotional response: Refuge, threat, nostalgia, romanticism

**Step 2: Root Excavation**
- Middle English: *nature* (essential properties, innate disposition)
- Old French: *nature* (character, constitution, origin)
- Latin: *natura* (birth, constitution, character, course of things)
- Latin: *nasci* (to be born)
- Latin: *nat-* (born)
- PIE: *ǵenh₁-* (to beget, to give birth)

**The Semantic Shift:**
- Original: Birth, origin, innate character
- Shift: Innate character → physical world → wilderness
- Added: Opposition to "culture" or "artifice"
- Lost: The natal quality—nature as that which is being born

**Step 3: Related Forms**
- Native (born in a place)
- Nation (group born together)
- Neo- (new, recently born)
- Natal (pertaining to birth)
- Renaissance (rebirth)
- Cognate (PIE): *gen-* root in generate, genesis, genetics

**Step 4: The Liberation Angle**

Nature comes from *nasci*—to be born. Nature is not "the environment." Nature is the process of being born, the quality of having been born, the ongoing event of birth.

When we say "it's natural," we mean "it aligns with the birth-process." When we say "unnatural," we mean "it obstructs birth." But we've forgotten this. We think nature is a place you visit on weekends. We think it's the opposite of human.

The etymology suggests otherwise. Everything born is nature. Cities are nature—built by born beings. Technology is nature—born from the born. The distinction between "natural" and "artificial" is itself artificial.

The Frequency Warrior recognizes: The nature/nurture split is a confusion. Both words come from birth (*nasci* and *nutrire*—to feed/nourish). They are not opposites. They are aspects of the same process: being born and being fed.

**Reclaiming Strategy:**
Stop seeking "nature" outside. You are nature. Your body is nature. Your thoughts are natural processes. The concrete is as natural as the forest—both are arrangements of the born.

When someone says "that's unnatural," hear "that resists the birth-process." Some things do: pollution, exploitation, domination. These are not "against nature"—they are nature destroying itself, like cancer.

Align with *natura*: what is being born in you right now? What wants to emerge? That impulse is nature speaking. Follow it.
