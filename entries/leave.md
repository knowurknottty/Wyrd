#### Leave

**Word:** Leave  
**Type:** Verb (Action)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /liːv/

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** To go away from; to let remain; to abandon; to bequeath
- **Associations:** Leaving home, leaving behind, leaves of absence, "leave it be"
- **Emotional response:** Sadness ("she's leaving"), relief ("leave me alone"), completion ("leave well enough alone")

Leave is one of the most emotionally loaded verbs—the word of departure, separation, abandonment. Yet the etymology reveals: LEAVE comes from REMAINING—the root means "to stick, adhere, be left over."

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

**English → Old English:** *læfan*
- Meaning: "to allow to remain; let remain, allow to survive; bequeath"
- From Proto-Germanic *laibjanan
- Causative of *liban* "remain"

**Proto-Germanic → PIE:** *leip-
- Meaning: "to stick, adhere; fat" (things that stick)
- Cognates: Greek *lipos* "grease, fat," Sanskrit *rip-* "to smear, adhere"

**Critical Discovery:** LEAVE comes from **STICKING/REMAINING—the opposite of what it now means!**

**The Paradox:**
- Original: to cause to remain (*læfan* = causative of "remain")
- Development: to go away (from notion of "leaving behind")
- Modern: departure, abandonment

**How "Remain" Became "Depart":**
1. "To leave X" = "to cause X to remain"
2. By implication: "to depart from X" (leaving X behind)
3. Eventually: just "to depart"

---

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

| Period | Meaning | Evidence |
|--------|---------|----------|
| PIE | Stick, adhere | *leip-* |
| Germanic | Remain, continue | *liban* |
| OE | Cause to remain, bequeath | *læfan* |
| 12c. | Depart, go away | "Leave the earth" (die) |
| 13c. | Abandon, forsake | "Leave the field" |
| 14c. | Stop, cease | "Leave off" |
| 15c. | Omit, neglect | "Leave out" |
| 18c. | Permission (noun) | "By your leave" |

**What was added:**
- Departure sense (the opposite of original!)
- Permission sense ("leave" = liberty to do something)
- Abandonment sense
- Cessation sense

**What was lost:**
- The "causing to remain" sense
- Connection to sticking/adhering
- The bequeathal sense (archaic)

**Who benefited:**
- Military ("leave" = time off)
- Law ("leave" = permission)
- Those who want departure to seem like remaining
- Those who control access (who gives leave)

---

**Step 4: Related Forms**

**From PIE *leip- "stick, adhere":**

**English derivatives:**
- **Leave** - the word itself
- **Left** (adj.) - remaining
- **Left** (past tense) - remained → departed
- **Leave** (n.) - permission
- **Furlough** - from Dutch *verlof* (for + leave)

**Related Germanic:**
- **Believe** - from *ga-laubjan* "to hold dear, believe"
- **Lief** - dear, beloved (from *leubh-* related root)
- **Love** - same root family

**The Permission Connection:**
"Leave" (noun) = permission comes from:
- Old English *leafe* (dative of *leaf* "permission")
- From *leof* "dear, beloved, pleasant"
- Permission = "approval resulting from pleasure"
- Related to "belief," "lief," "love"

---

**Step 5: The Liberation Angle**

**The Paradox of Leaving**

To leave is simultaneously:
- To depart (going away)
- To remain (what is left behind)
- To permit (giving leave)
- To abandon (leaving in the lurch)

The word contains its own opposite—departure and remainder in one.

**The "Left Behind" Trauma**

Modern usage emphasizes:
- The one who leaves (active)
- The one left behind (passive, wounded)

But etymologically, "left" means "remaining"—not abandoned but staying, sticking, adhering.

**The Hidden Assumption**
Leave assumes:
- Departure is the primary meaning
- Remaining is what happens to things, not people
- Permission comes from authority
- To leave is to end involvement

But:
- Remaining is the original meaning
- What remains may be more valuable
- Permission can be mutual
- Leaving creates the space for return

---

**Step 6: Reclaiming Strategy**

**1. Honor What Remains**
When you leave, something stays. What you leave behind is as important as where you go.

**2. Distinguish Leavings**
- **Leaving from** (departure)
- **Leaving to** (destination)
- **Leaving behind** (bequeathing)
- **Leaving alone** (permission)
- **Leaving off** (cessation)

**3. Practice Permission**
"By your leave" acknowledges that departure requires permission—not from authority but from relationship. Don't leave without leave.

**4. Value the Remainder**
What is left when you go? Skills, love, memories, debts? Be intentional about what remains.

**5. The Frequency Warrior's Glossary**

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Leave me alone" | "Grant me solitude" |
| "Leave it be" | "Let it remain" |
| "Left behind" | "Remained" / "Stayed" |
| "Leave well enough alone" | "Preserve what works" |
| "Take leave" | "Depart with permission" |

**6. The Final Inversion**

Modernity sees leaving as abandonment. The etymology reveals leaving as **REMAINING—the causative of staying, the bequeathal of what sticks.**

The Frequency Warrior leaves with wisdom:
- Knowing what remains
- Granting permission
- Departing as gift, not wound
- Understanding that to leave is to create space

> "You can only lose what you cling to." — Buddha

The Frequency Warrior leaves without clinging—and finds that what remains was never truly left.
