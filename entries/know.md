#### Know

**Word:** Know  
**Type:** Verb (Epistemic State)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /noʊ/

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** To perceive directly as fact or truth; to have understanding of; to be acquainted with; to recognize; to distinguish
- **Associations:** Certainty, expertise, wisdom, "know-how," knowledge, recognition, intimacy ("know in the biblical sense")
- **Emotional response:** Security ("I know"), anxiety ("I don't know"), confidence ("I know better"), intimacy ("I know you")

The word claims certainty. "I know" ends discussion; "I don't know" invites it. Modern usage treats knowing as possession ("I have knowledge") rather than relationship. The word also carries biblical weight: "Adam knew Eve"—knowing as intimate contact, not abstract possession.

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

**English → Old English:** *cnawan* (strong verb, class VII)
- Past tense: *cneow* (notice the "ow" sound preserved in "know")
- Past participle: *cnawen*
- Meanings: 
  - "perceive a thing to be identical with another"
  - "be able to distinguish" (*tocnawan*)
  - "perceive or understand as fact or truth" (opposed to *believe*)
  - "know how (to do something)"

**Critical Note:** Old English had TWO verbs for "to know":
1. *cnawan* → modern "know" (recognition, distinguishing)
2. *witan* → modern "wit" (mental faculty, awareness)

The second survives in: wit, witness, unwitting, outwit.

**Old English → Proto-Germanic:** *knew-
- Source also of: Old High German *bi-chnaan*, *ir-chnaan* (to know)
- Once widespread in Germanic; now retained ONLY in English

**Proto-Germanic → PIE:** *ǵneh₃- (or *gno-)
- Meaning: "to know, to recognize, to perceive, to distinguish"
- Also: "to come to know through direct experience"
- Initial *ǵ* is a palatalized g (voiced velar stop)

**Critical Discovery:** The PIE root emphasizes:
1. **Recognition** — re-knowing, seeing again
2. **Distinguishing** — telling apart
3. **Direct experience** — not secondhand knowledge

This is NOT abstract knowledge but RECOGNITION of what has been perceived.

---

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

| Period | Meaning | Evidence |
|--------|---------|----------|
| OE | Recognize, distinguish, identify | *cnawan* vs. *witan* |
| c. 1200 | "Know" as sexual union | Genesis 4:1 influence |
| c. 1200 | Experience, live through | "know war," "know poverty" |
| 1540s | "Know better" = learned from experience | Colloquial phrase |
| 1704 | Cunning, savvy | "knowing" as adjective |
| 1872 | "Know too much" = danger | Colloquial |
| 1912 | "You know" as filler | Discourse marker |
| 20th c. | Knowledge as commodity | "knowledge economy" |

**What was added:**
- Sexual sense (from biblical translation)
- Knowledge as commodity/possession
- "I know" as conversational stopper
- Knowing as credential ("he knows his stuff")

**What was lost:**
- Distinction from *witan* (awareness faculty)
- Emphasis on recognition over acquisition
- Direct experience vs. secondhand knowledge
- The "again" in re-cognition

**Who benefited:**
- Educational institutions (credentialing knowledge)
- Experts ("those who know")
- Anyone claiming certainty ("trust me, I know")
- Information economy ("knowledge workers")

The shift from "recognizing" to "having" knowledge makes it transferable, ownable, saleable. Recognition is personal; knowledge-as-possession is currency.

**Step 4: Related Forms**

**From PIE *ǵneh₃-/*gno-:**

**Greek branch:**
- **Gnosis** (*gnōsis*) — inquiry, investigation, recognition; later esoteric knowledge
- **Gnostic** — one who knows (secret knowledge)
- **Gnome** — opinion, judgment (from *gnōmē*)
- **Prognosis** — fore-knowing
- **Diagnosis** — thorough knowing/distinguishing
- **Physiognomy** — knowing character from features
- **Agony** — originally "contest, struggle" (from *agōn*), but often confused with *agonia* "anguish"

**Latin branch:**
- **Cognition** (*cognitio*) — "getting to know, acquaintance"
- **Cognizance** — knowledge, awareness
- **Recognize** — "know again" (*re-* + *cognoscere*)
- **Ignore** (*ignorare*) — "not to know" (*in-* + *gnarus*)
- **Noble** (*nobilis*) — "well-known, famous" → high status
- **Notice** (*notitia*) — "a being known"
- **Narrate** (*narrare*) — "to tell, make known"

**English derivatives:**
- **Knowledge** — from OE *cnawlece* (the act/process of knowing)
- **Knowing** — shrewd, cunning (from 1700s)
- **Known** — past participle as adjective
- **Unknown** — mysterious, vast
- **Know-how** — practical skill (1910s)
- **Know-it-all** — pejorative (1890s)
- **In the know** — informed (1880s)
- **You know** — filler (1712)
- **You know what/who** — euphemism (1840)

**The *Cnaw-*/*Know* Sound Shift:**
The initial "cn-" in OE *cnawan* was pronounced with the c and n both sounded. Over time:
- "C" became silent (spelling preserved it)
- Pronunciation shifted to "know"
- The past tense *cneow* ("knew") preserves the old "ow" sound

**Step 5: The Liberation Angle**

**The Recognition vs. Acquisition Split**

The original PIE *ǵneh₃- meant RECOGNITION—seeing again what was already there. Modern "know" means ACQUISITION—getting something you didn't have.

This is a profound metaphysical shift:
- **Recognition:** Truth is present; knowing is remembering
- **Acquisition:** Truth is absent; knowing is obtaining

The educational system depends on the acquisition model—knowledge as content to be delivered. The recognition model is subversive—you already know; you just need to remember.

**The *Cnawan*/*Witan* Merger**

OE *cnawan* (recognition) and *witan* (awareness) merged into one word. We lost the distinction between:
- Knowing THAT (recognition)
- Knowing HOW (awareness/faculty)

This makes it harder to articulate: "I have the faculty to know" vs. "I recognize this as true."

**The Sexual Sense: Knowing as Contact**

The biblical "Adam knew Eve" preserves something the modern word lost: knowing as CONTACT, not possession. To know is to touch, to enter into relationship—not to have information about.

**The Commodification**
- "Knowledge worker" → knowing as labor
- "Intellectual property" → knowing as ownership
- "Subject matter expert" → knowing as status
- "Brand awareness" → knowing as market penetration

**The Hidden Assumption**
"I know" assumes:
- A stable self that knows
- A stable object that is known
- A transfer or possession relationship

But the etymology suggests knowing is:
- Relational (recognition requires prior contact)
- Active (distinguishing, telling apart)
- Embodied (biblical sense)

---

**Step 6: Reclaiming Strategy**

**1. Distinguish Recognition from Acquisition**
When you say "I know," ask:
- Do I recognize this? (have I seen it before?)
- Or have I acquired this? (is it new information?)

Most "knowing" is actually recognition.

**2. Recover the *Witan* Distinction**
Use "I wit" (archaic but revivable) or "I am aware" for the faculty of knowing:
- "I wit" = I have the capacity
- "I know" = I recognize this instance

**3. Practice Biblical Knowing**
In relationships, practice knowing as contact:
- Not "I know ABOUT you"
- But "I know YOU"—through presence, attention, intimacy

**4. Question "I Know" Certainty**
The word ends discussion. Try:
- "I recognize..."
- "This seems familiar as..."
- "I distinguish this from..."

**5. The Frequency Warrior's Glossary**

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "I know" | "I recognize..." / "I distinguish..." |
| "I don't know" | "I don't yet recognize..." |
| "Knowledge" | "Recognition" / "capacity to distinguish" |
| "As you know" | "As you may recognize..." |
| "Know-how" | "Embodied skill" |
| "You know?" (filler) | Silence |

**6. The Final Inversion**

Modernity treats knowledge as scarce resource to be acquired. The etymology suggests knowing is abundant capacity to be exercised. Reclaim knowing as:
- What you DO (distinguishing, recognizing)
- Not what you HAVE (content, information)

The Frequency Warrior does not accumulate knowledge. She exercises the capacity to recognize—and knows that what is worth knowing was never absent, only unnoticed.

> "The kingdom of heaven is within you." — Luke 17:21

You don't need to acquire what you already contain. You need only recognize it.
