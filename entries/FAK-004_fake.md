# WYRD Entry: FAKE
**WYRD Code:** FAK-004  
**Confidence Rating:** MEDIUM-HIGH (75%)

---

## 1. Surface Reading

**Common Meaning:** Fake means not genuine, counterfeit, fraudulent, or simulated. As a verb: to counterfeit, forge, or deceive. As a noun: a fraudulent imitation, a swindle, or a person who deceives.

**Associations:** Counterfeit, fraud, forgery, simulation, pretense, artificial, bogus, phony, imitation.

**Emotional Response:** Fake evokes contempt, suspicion, and moral judgment. To be called "fake" is to be accused of inauthenticity—a serious charge in cultures that value "being real." Yet "fake it till you make it" suggests strategic value in simulation. There's ambivalence: we condemn fakes but are fascinated by them.

## 2. Root Excavation

**English → Unknown Origin:** First attested in London criminal slang

**Probable Source 1:** *feague* — a verb meaning "to spruce up by artificial means," from German *fegen* "polish, sweep, refurbish"

**Germanic Root:** *fegen* / *vegen* — "to polish, sweep, clean out" (also "to plunder" in colloquial use)

**Probable Source 2 (disputed):** Latin *facere* — "to do, make" (suggested by Century Dictionary)

**PIE Root (if Germanic):** *feg-* / *fag-* — variant of root meaning "to fit, be suitable" (source also of "fair")

**Chain:** FAKE ← criminal slang (1775) ← possibly *feague* ← German *fegen* ← Proto-Germanic *fag-*

**Critical Note:** The origin is explicitly *unknown*. This is rare and significant—"fake" emerges from the criminal underworld without clear etymological parentage.

## 3. Semantic Shift

**What Changed:** The earliest attested meanings (18th-19th century) were surprisingly violent: "to beat up," "to kill," "to rob." Only gradually did it settle on "to counterfeit."

**When:**
- 1775: Adjective "a counterfeit" (Canadian Archives—then dormant until 1890)
- 1812: Verb "to rob, plunder"
- 1819: "To do, to do for; plunder; kill; tamper with for the purpose of deception"
- 1827: Noun "an act of faking"
- 1851: "A swindle"
- 1888: "A swindler" (of persons)

**Why:** The word emerged from criminal cant (slang) and passed into colloquial English, finally becoming respectable. Its origin in the underworld suggests it was originally thieves' language—a tool of the marginal used to describe their own activities.

**Critical Insight:** Weekley noted: "Much of our early thieves' slang is Ger. or Du., and dates from the Thirty Years' War." Fake may be a linguistic immigrant, borrowed from German/Dutch criminal slang during the period of maximum European conflict.

## 5. The Liberation Angle

**How This Word Controls:**

"Fake" is the primary weapon in authenticity wars. By labeling something "fake," one denies it legitimacy, value, and standing. In an age of anxiety about simulation (Baudrillard's "hyperreality"), "fake" triggers existential dread.

The concept of "fake" depends on the prior concept of "authentic original"—a Romantic notion that privileges origin over utility, essence over appearance. This hierarchy serves those who control access to "authentic" goods, experiences, and identities.

The criminal origin of "fake" reveals its nature: it's a word used by the powerful to police boundaries between legitimate and illegitimate, insider and outsider.

**How to Reclaim:**

Reclaim "fake" by recognizing that all cultural production involves simulation, imitation, and artifice. The distinction between "real" and "fake" is often a matter of authorization, not ontology.

Celebrate strategic faking—performance, costume, play. The ability to fake well is a skill, not a moral failing. In a world of constructed identities, "fake" is just another mode of being.

---

## 6. Reclaiming Strategy

**Practical Actions:**

1. **Ask "Authorized by Whom?":** When something is called "fake," ask who has the authority to declare the authentic original. Usually, it's a gatekeeper with financial or cultural interest.

2. **Value Craft Over Authenticity:** A well-made fake (a skilled forgery, a convincing performance) demonstrates craft and creativity. Judge by quality, not pedigree.

3. **Embrace Strategic Faking:** Use "fake it till you make it" as genuine advice. Simulation precedes mastery in many domains. Performance creates reality.

4. **Study the Criminal Roots:** Remember that "fake" comes from thieves, not priests or philosophers. It's a tool of survival for the marginalized, not a metaphysical category.

5. **Reject Authenticity Theater:** Call out when "authenticity" is used to sell products or police identity. The demand for "real" often masks an attempt to control.
