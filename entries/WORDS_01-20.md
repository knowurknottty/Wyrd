## Thematic Clusters

### Truth & Falsity (Entries 1-4)
- **Truth** (TRU-001): Original meaning was *faithfulness/loyalty* (like a tree), not correspondence to fact
- **False** (FAL-002): Foreign Latin import replacing native words; used for religious/political control
- **Real** (REA-003): Originally *legal* terminology—"relating to things (property)"
- **Fake** (FAK-004): Unknown origin in criminal slang; emerged from underworld

### Emotion & Conflict (Entries 5-8)
- **Love** (LOV-005): PIE root *leubh-* gives us both *love* and *libido*
- **Hate** (HAT-006): Same PIE root (*keh₂d-*) yields both hate AND grief/care
- **War** (WAR-007): Originally meant *confusion/mixture*; no common Germanic word for war existed
- **Peace** (PEA-008): From *binding together* (PIE *pag-*); same root gives us *pay* and *pact*

### Existence (Entries 9-10)
- **Life** (LIF-009): From *sticking/adhering*; related to *liver* and *leave* (remain)
- **Death** (DEA-010): Remarkably stable meaning; Old English plural meant *ghosts*

### Activity (Entries 11-12)
- **Work** (WRK-011): Same root (*werg-*) gives us *organ*, *energy*, *orgy*—all "work"
- **Play** (PLY-012): Originally meant "being occupied"; opposed to work only since 14th c.

### Technology & Ontology (Entries 13-16)
- **Machine** (MAC-013): From *that which enables* (PIE *magh-*); same root as *may/might*
- **Human** (HUM-014): From *earthling* (PIE *dhghem-*); same root as *humus*, *humble*
- **Natural** (NAT-015): From *birth* (PIE *gene-*); same root as *gene*, *nation*, *native*
- **Artificial** (ART-016): From *art* + *making*; originally positive (skilled craft)

### Social Order (Entries 17-20)
- **Public** (PUB-017): From *the people* (Latin *populus*); originally "of the state"
- **Private** (PRI-018): From *to deprive/free*; private = separated from the public
- **Legal** (LEG-019): Latin import alongside Germanic *law*; Norman/French conquest legacy
- **Illegal** (ILL-020): Simple negation; weaponized when applied to persons rather than acts

## Cross-Cutting Insights

### Foreign Invasions
Several key words are Latin/French imports that displaced native Germanic terms:
- **false** replaced Old English *ā-lēogan*
- **war** is Frankish/French import (no native Germanic word existed)
- **real**, **legal**, **public**, **private** are all Latin/French

This reveals the Norman Conquest's profound impact on English conceptual vocabulary.

### PIE Root Clusters
- *deru-* (tree/firm): **truth**, **trust**, **tree**
- *leubh-* (care/love): **love**, **libido**, **belief** (archaic *lief*)
- *leip-* (stick/adhere): **life**, **liver**, **leave** (remain)
- *magh-* (be able): **machine**, **may**, **might**
- *dhghem-* (earth): **human**, **humble**, **humus**, **exhume**
- *gene-* (birth): **natural**, **nation**, **gene**, **native**, **pregnant**

### Semantic Inversions
- **truth**: loyalty → factual accuracy
- **war**: confusion → organized military conflict
- **fake**: violent crime ("to kill") → counterfeit
- **play**: occupation → recreation opposite work

## Liberation Themes Summary

### How Words Control
1. **Monopolizing definitions** (truth, real, natural)
2. **Foreign imposition** (false, legal, war)
3. **Binary enforcement** (public/private, legal/illegal, natural/artificial)
4. **Moral loading** (illegal, false, artificial)
5. **Depoliticization** (peace as mere absence of war)

### Reclaiming Strategies
1. **Return to etymological roots** (human = earthling, peace = binding)
2. **Reject false binaries** (natural/artificial, public/private)
3. **Honor criminal/marginal origins** (fake, machine as cunning)
4. **Distinguish legal from just** (legal ≠ right)
5. **Expand narrow modern senses** (work, play, truth)
