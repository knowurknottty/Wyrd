#### Truth

**Word:** Truth  
**Type:** Noun (Concept)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /truːθ/

**Step 1: Surface Reading**
- Common meaning: That which is in accordance with fact or reality
- Associations: Honesty, accuracy, revelation, justice, dogma
- Emotional response: Relief, exposure, fear, certainty, doubt

**Step 2: Root Excavation**
- Middle English: *treuth* (faith, loyalty, honesty)
- Old English: *triewth* (faith, fidelity, loyalty)
- Old English: *treowe* (faithful, trustworthy)
- Proto-Germanic: *trewwaz* (having faith, loyal)
- PIE: *dru-* (firm, solid, steadfast)
- Related to tree (firm, solid growth) and trust

**The Semantic Shift:**
- Original: Faithfulness, loyalty, steadfastness
- Shift: Personal fidelity → factual accuracy → correspondence with reality
- Added: Epistemological abstraction (truth as property of statements)
- Lost: The relational quality—truth as trustworthiness

**Step 3: Related Forms**
- Trust (firm reliance)
- Tree (that which stands firm)
- Truce (agreement of trust)
- Troth (pledge, fidelity—"plight one's troth")
- Druid (Celtic: *dru-*, one who is steadfast/firm)

**Step 4: The Liberation Angle**

Truth comes from the same root as tree and trust. Originally, truth was not "accuracy"—it was *steadfastness*. To be true was to be faithful, reliable, solid as an oak.

"True love" preserves this: not "accurate love" but *faithful* love. "True friend"—a loyal friend. "True north"—the reliable direction.

The philosophical shift turned truth from a quality of persons into a quality of propositions. "Is it true?" became "Does it correspond to reality?" But the etymology asks: "Is it faithful? Can it be trusted? Does it stand firm?"

The Frequency Warrior recognizes: Truth as correspondence creates anxiety (how do we know it corresponds?). Truth as steadfastness creates peace (can I trust this? is it faithful?).

**Reclaiming Strategy:**
Ask not "Is this true?" but "Is this faithful?" "Can I trust this?" "Does it stand firm under testing?"

Be true—not "be accurate" but *be faithful*. To what? To your word. To your path. To those you love. This is the old truth, the tree-truth, the trust-truth.

When someone claims to have "the Truth," remember: they have a proposition. But truth (*treowe*) is a quality of persons. Only persons can be true. Ideas can only be consistent—or not.
