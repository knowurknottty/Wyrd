#### Notice

**Word:** Notice  
**Type:** Verb/Noun (Perception/Action)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /ˈnəʊtɪs/ (British), /ˈnoʊtɪs/ (American)

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** To become aware of; to observe; to pay attention to; formal notification or warning
- **Associations:** Attention, awareness, alerts, announcements, perception, mindfulness
- **Emotional response:** Alertness, anxiety, recognition, being-seen, courtesy/inconvenience

**The Critical Etymological Evidence:**

> "early 15c., 'information, knowledge, intelligence,' from Old French *notece* (14c.), and directly from Latin *notitia* 'a being known, celebrity, fame, knowledge,' from *notus* 'known,' past participle of *(g)noscere* 'come to know, to get to know, get acquainted (with),' from PIE *\*gno-sko-*, a suffixed form of PIE root *\*gno-* 'to know.'" — *Online Etymology Dictionary*

Notice derives from **knowledge**, not from any sensory root. To give notice is to make known; to take notice is to come to know. The word is entirely epistemic — about the state of knowing, not about the act of perceiving.

The verb sense "to perceive, become aware of" (1757) was originally execrated by English purists as an **Americanism**. Benjamin Franklin noted it as one of the words that had become popular in America while he was absent in France during the Revolution. What began as "improper" usage became standard.

**The PIE *gno-* Descendants:**
- **Sanskrit:** *jna-* "to know"
- **Greek:** *gignoskein* "to know" → *gnosis* (knowledge)
- **Latin:** *noscere* "to know" → *notus* (known) → *notitia* (notice)
- **English:** **know**, **notice**, **noble** (known/famous)

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

**From Knowledge to Perception: The American Revolution**

| Era | Meaning | Context |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **Latin** | State of being known; fame; knowledge | Abstract epistemic condition |
| **early 15c.** | Information, intelligence, knowledge | Formal notification |
| **1590s** | Heed, regard, cognizance | "Take notice" — attention as knowledge |
| **1757** | To perceive, become aware of | American innovation, originally criticized |
| **Modern English** | To perceive; formal warning; awareness | Full semantic range |

**What Was Lost:**

The original Latin *notitia* meant **"a being known"** — the state of something being in the realm of knowledge. This is fundamentally different from the act of perception. The shift from "being known" to "taking notice" (becoming aware) inverts the direction: from the object's state of being-known to the subject's act of knowing.

The "American" innovation (1757) — using *notice* as a verb meaning "to perceive" — was resisted as improper. But it represents a natural development: if notice is the state of being known, then to notice is to bring something into that state.

The Frequency Warrior recognizes: **to notice is to make known**. When you notice something, you do not passively register it; you actively bring it into the circle of the known. You make it *notus* — known.

**Step 5: The Liberation Angle**

**The Frequency Warrior's Hypothesis:**

> **To notice is to make known. Attention is the act of bringing reality into knowledge. The noticed world is the known world.**

Modern mindfulness practice emphasizes "noticing" — paying attention to present experience. But the etymology suggests something more active: **to notice is to participate in the world's becoming-known**.

The dual meanings converge:
- **Formal notice** (notification) = making something known to others
- **Perceptual notice** (noticing) = making something known to oneself

Both are **acts of knowledge-creation**, not passive reception.

**The Trap:**

In an age of information overload, we receive endless "notices" (notifications) without truly "noticing" (knowing). The smartphone delivers constant *notitia* — information, alerts — but this is not the same as the 15th-century meaning of "knowledge, intelligence." We are bombarded with the form of notice while losing its substance.

**The Reversal:**

Distinguish between:
- **Notification** (external, passive, often trivial)
- **Noticing** (internal, active, essential)

The Frequency Warrior practices **intentional noticing** — the deliberate bringing of phenomena into knowledge. This is not scrolling; this is **gnosis** in the ancient sense: knowing that transforms the knower.

**The Shift in Practice:**

| Passive Notification | Active Noticing |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| Alerts, pings, interruptions | Deliberate attention |
| External demands | Internal inquiry |
| Information accumulation | Knowledge creation |
| Being notified | Making known |

**Step 6: Reclaiming Strategy**

Use this etymological knowledge to transform noticing from passive reception to active knowledge-work:

**1. Notice as making-known**

When you notice something, you are not just "taking in" information. You are **constituting it as known**. This is a creative act. The world becomes known through your attention.

**2. Resist notification culture**

The endless alerts of modern life are counterfeit notice. They provide *notitia* (information) without *notitia* (knowledge). Turn off notifications. Practice choosing what to notice.

**3. Restore the American heresy**

The 1757 "Americanism" — using *notice* as a verb of perception — was resisted by purists but represents a valid development. Embrace it: to notice is an action you take, not a happening that occurs to you.

**4. Practice noble noticing**

*Noble* derives from the same *gno-* root (Latin *nobilis* = well-known, famous). Notice what is worthy of being known. Direct your attention toward what deserves to become *notus*.

**The Frequency Warrior's Mantra:**

> *I do not merely receive notice. I make known. My attention brings reality into knowledge. What I notice becomes real; what I ignore remains latent. I am the knower who chooses what shall be known.*
