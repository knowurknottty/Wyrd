# WYRD Entry: WORK
**WYRD Code:** WRK-011  
**Confidence Rating:** HIGH (87%)

---

## 1. Surface Reading

**Common Meaning:** Work is activity involving mental or physical effort done to achieve a purpose or result; employment, labor, occupation. As a verb: to perform work, to function, to labor.

**Associations:** Labor, effort, production, employment, career, craft, toil, achievement, purpose.

**Emotional Response:** Work evokes mixed feelings—pride and resentment, purpose and exhaustion. Modern "work ethic" transforms work from necessity to virtue/identity. The word carries Puritan and capitalist baggage.

## 3. Semantic Shift

**What Changed:** The original sense was broader than modern "employment"—*weorc* meant any deed, act, or thing made. The narrowing to "paid employment" is recent.

**When:**
- Old English: "something done, action, that which is made, physical labor, skilled trade, military fortification"
- c. 1200: "physical effort, exertion"
- c. 1200: "scholarly labor" and "artistic labor"
- c. 1300: "labor as a measurable commodity"
- 1590s: "out of work" (unemployed)

**Why:** The commodification of labor (c. 1300) reflects the rise of market economies. Work became measurable, exchangeable, separable from the worker. The "work ethic" (1959) represents the internalization of this commodification.

**Critical Insight:** Old English *weorc* also meant "fornication"—suggesting work and sexual activity were conceptually linked as fundamental human activities. The Puritan separation of "work" (virtuous) from "pleasure" (suspect) obscures this connection.

## 5. The Liberation Angle

**How This Word Controls:**

"Work" is the central mechanism of capitalist control. The demand that we "work"—produce value for others—is enforced through economic coercion and ideological manipulation ("work ethic," "contributing member of society").

The narrowing of *weorc* (any deed) to "paid employment" devalues unpaid care work, artistic creation, political activity, and leisure. What doesn't pay doesn't count as "work."

The etymological connection to *organ* and *energy* reveals work as fundamental activity—not punishment but capacity. Capitalism transforms capacity into compulsion.

**How to Reclaim:**

Reclaim work by returning to the PIE root: *werg-* = "to do." All doing is work—cooking, gardening, loving, playing, resisting. Expand work beyond employment.

Honor *weorc* as "something made, something done." Value process over product, craft over commodity, intrinsic satisfaction over external reward.

---

## 6. Reclaiming Strategy

**Practical Actions:**

1. **Expand the Definition:** Count care work, creative work, political work, and self-work as *real work*. Reject the narrowing to paid employment.

2. **Honor Craft:** The Old English sense included "skilled trade, craft." Rediscover craft—making things well for their own sake, not for market value.

3. **Study the Greek Connection:** *Ergon* gives us *energy, organ, orgy*. Work is fundamental to being. Don't let capitalism monopolize it.

4. **Question the Work Ethic:** The "work ethic" (1959) is recent ideological construction. It serves employers, not workers. Distinguish work from exploitation.

5. **Work Less:** The ultimate reclamation may be working less. If work is fundamental capacity, we need periods of non-work—rest, play, idleness—to restore that capacity.
