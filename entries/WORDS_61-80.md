## Overview

This batch explores four interconnected domains:

1. **Cognitive Processes** (61-63): The mechanisms of mind—think, feel, know
2. **Educational Systems** (64-70): Learning, teaching, and institutional power—learn, teach, study, school, student, teacher, master
3. **Power & Constraint** (71-76): Freedom and its opposite—slave, free, bind, chain, lock, key
4. **Thresholds** (77-80): Passages and boundaries—door, open, close, enter

These words form a narrative arc: from internal mental processes, through institutional conditioning, through systems of control, to the possibilities of passage and transformation.

## The Words

### 61. THINK
- **PIE Root:** *tong-/*teng- "to think, feel"
- **Original Sense:** "To cause to appear to oneself" (active) vs. "to seem" (passive)
- **Key Discovery:** Two Old English verbs merged, losing the distinction between active thinking and passive seeming
- **Liberation:** Reclaim thinking as event, not identity; distinguish "I construct" from "it seems"

### 62. FEEL
- **PIE Root:** *pal- "to touch lightly, vibrate"
- **Original Sense:** Light touch, stroking, sensory contact
- **Key Discovery:** Feeling evolved from tactile to emotional; originally "light touch," not force
- **Liberation:** Return feeling to sensory contact; feel lightly, not forcefully

### 63. KNOW
- **PIE Root:** *ǵneh₃-/*gno- "to know, recognize, distinguish"
- **Original Sense:** Recognition, re-knowing, direct experience
- **Key Discovery:** Knowing was originally recognition of what is present, not acquisition of absent information
- **Liberation:** Trust direct perception over credentialized expertise

### 64. LEARN
- **PIE Root:** *leis- "track, furrow"
- **Original Sense:** "To follow or find the track"
- **Key Discovery:** Learn and "last" are cognate—learning requires endurance, following the path
- **Liberation:** Learn as navigation, not consumption; follow tracks, don't just receive content

### 65. TEACH
- **PIE Root:** *deik- "to show, point out"
- **Original Sense:** To show, point out, give signs/tokens
- **Key Discovery:** Teacher = pointer, not vessel; teaching is revelation, not delivery
- **Liberation:** Point to what is present; don't claim to possess secret knowledge

### 66. STUDY
- **PIE Root:** *(s)teu- "to push, stick, knock, beat"
- **Original Sense:** "To be thrusting forward, eager"
- **Key Discovery:** Study was originally aggressive—pressing forward with eagerness, not passive desk work
- **Liberation:** Study with zeal and drive, not just dutiful compliance

### 67. SCHOOL
- **Greek Origin:** *skholē* "leisure, free time, rest"
- **Original Sense:** Leisure for learned discussion (opposite of work)
- **Key Discovery:** School originally meant LEISURE—the opposite of modern compulsory schooling
- **Liberation:** Reclaim learning as leisure, not obligation; *skholē* as freedom

### 68. STUDENT
- **Latin Root:** *studere* "to be eager, apply oneself"
- **Original Sense:** One who thrusts forward eagerly
- **Key Discovery:** Student = active pursuer, not passive recipient of teaching
- **Liberation:** Be a student of life, not just of institutions; maintain eagerness regardless of age

### 69. TEACHER
- **PIE Root:** *deik- "to show, point out" (same as teach)
- **Original Sense:** One who points out, shows the way
- **Key Discovery:** Teacher as pointer, not authority possessing truth
- **Liberation:** Point toward truth; don't claim ownership of it

### 70. MASTER
- **PIE Root:** *meg- "great"
- **Original Sense:** "The greater one" (comparative, not absolute)
- **Key Discovery:** Mastery was contextual greatness, not absolute dominance; originally included teaching
- **Liberation:** Seek mastery of self and craft, not domination over others

### 71. SLAVE
- **Etymology:** From "Slav" (ethnic name)—Slavic people were so frequently enslaved their name became synonymous with bondage
- **Original Sense:** Slavic person; "the famous people" (*kleu-* "renown")
- **Key Discovery:** The word embodies linguistic violence—ethnic erasure through enslavement
- **Liberation:** Remember the Slavic origin; honor the "famous"; abolish all bondage

### 72. FREE
- **PIE Root:** *pri- "to love"
- **Original Sense:** "Beloved; not in bondage"—free because belonging
- **Key Discovery:** FREE and FRIEND are doublets—freedom is belonging, not autonomy
- **Liberation:** Seek freedom in connection, not isolation

### 73. BIND
- **PIE Root:** *bhendh- "to bind"
- **Original Sense:** To make connection, join together
- **Key Discovery:** Bind gives us BAND, BOND, BUNDLE—all forms of connection
- **Liberation:** Distinguish imprisoning bonds from connecting bonds; value right binding

### 74. CHAIN
- **Possible PIE Root:** *kat- "to twist, twine"
- **Original Sense:** Twisted connection; entanglement like hunting net
- **Key Discovery:** Same word for slavery's chains and commerce's chains (chain stores)
- **Liberation:** Remember the entanglement; distinguish connection from capture

### 75. LOCK
- **Proto-Germanic:** *luk- "to close, bend"
- **Original Sense:** Enclosure; barrier
- **Key Discovery:** Lock creates inside/outside; same mechanism protects or imprisons
- **Liberation:** Question every lock—who is protected, who imprisoned?

### 76. KEY
- **Probable PIE Root:** *klau- "hook, crook"
- **Original Sense:** Hooked tool for lifting lock mechanisms
- **Key Discovery:** Key and "close" share root (*clavis* = key); keys are hooks that engage
- **Liberation:** Keys are simple tools; question who controls access

### 77. DOOR
- **PIE Root:** *dhwer- "door, doorway, outside"
- **Original Sense:** Passage to the outside; the exterior (forum was "outside")
- **Key Discovery:** Door originally marked passage TO public space, not just protection FROM it
- **Liberation:** Open doors to participation, not just close them for protection

### 78. OPEN
- **PIE Root:** *upo "under, up from under"
- **Original Sense:** "Raised up, set upright" (not closed down)
- **Key Discovery:** OPEN and UP are related; opening is active lifting, not passive state
- **Liberation:** Opening requires work; lift up what's hidden

### 79. CLOSE
- **PIE Root:** *klau- "hook, crook, bar"
- **Original Sense:** To secure with hooked bar or bolt
- **Key Discovery:** CLOSE and KEY share root (*claudere*/*clavis*); closing is barring
- **Liberation:** Closing protects and completes; don't fear necessary endings

### 80. ENTER
- **PIE Root:** *en "in" + comparative *enter "between, among"
- **Original Sense:** To go within; to move among
- **Key Discovery:** ENTER bridges *inter* (between) and *intra* (within)—relationship and interiority
- **Liberation:** Enter with full presence; honor thresholds; move toward center

## Thematic Connections

### The Cognition-Constraint Arc

Words 61-80 trace a disturbing pattern:

1. **Internal processes** (think, feel, know) become...
2. **Institutionalized learning** (school, student, teacher) which creates...
3. **Systems of power** (master/slave, free/bind, chain/lock) that control...
4. **Access to thresholds** (door, open, close, enter)

The arc suggests: cognition is captured by education, which is enforced by power, which controls passage.

### The Inversion Pattern

Several words underwent semantic inversions:
- **School:** Leisure → Obligation
- **Slave:** Ethnic name (*Slav* = "famous") → Bondage
- **Free:** Belonging → Autonomy
- **Student:** Active pursuer → Passive recipient

These inversions reveal systems of control capturing natural human capacities.

### The *Klau- Cluster

PIE *klau- "hook" connects:
- **Key** (hooks the lock)
- **Close** (*claudere* = to close)
- **Clavicle** (collarbone = "little key")
- **Include/Exclude/Conclude** (all "shutting")

The hook/bolt metaphor runs through control mechanisms.

### The *En-/*Inter-/*Intra- Cluster

PIE *en "in" connects:
- **Enter** (go within/among)
- **Interior** (within)
- **Inter** (between)
- **Intimate** (inmost)

Entering bridges inner depth and outer relationship.

## Liberation Insights

### Reclaiming Cognition
- **Think:** Distinguish active construction from passive seeming
- **Feel:** Return to sensory contact, not emotional abstraction
- **Know:** Trust direct recognition over credentialized expertise

### Reclaiming Education
- **Learn:** Follow tracks, don't consume content
- **Teach:** Point to what's present, don't deliver absence
- **Study:** Thrust forward eagerly, don't sit passively
- **School:** Learning as leisure (*skholē*), not obligation

### Reclaiming Freedom
- **Free:** Belonging and friendship, not isolation
- **Bind:** Right connection, not constraint
- **Slave:** Remember the Slavic origin; abolish all bondage

### Reclaiming Thresholds
- **Open:** Active lifting, not passive state
- **Close:** Protection and completion, not just ending
- **Enter:** Full presence at thresholds
- **Door:** Passage to participation, not just protection
