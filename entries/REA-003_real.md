# WYRD Entry: REAL
**WYRD Code:** REA-003  
**Confidence Rating:** HIGH (88%)

---

## 1. Surface Reading

**Common Meaning:** Real means actually existing, genuine, authentic, not imaginary or artificial. It connotes materiality, verifiability, and substantial presence. In common parlance, "get real" means face facts; "the real thing" means authentic, not counterfeit.

**Associations:** Authenticity, material existence, truth, substance, seriousness, legitimacy.

**Emotional Response:** Real evokes solidity, trustworthiness, groundedness. To be called "real" is high praise—it implies someone is not performing, not fake, genuinely themselves. "Real" carries a positive valence against the anxiety of simulation and artificiality in modern life.

## 2. Root Excavation

**English → Middle English:** *real* — from Old French *reel*, from Late Latin *realis*

**Late Latin:** *realis* — "actual, belonging to the thing itself"

**Latin:** *res* — "property, goods, matter, thing, affair"

**PIE Root:** *Hreh-i-* — "wealth, goods, property"

**Chain:** REAL ← Old French *reel* ← Late Latin *realis* ← Latin *res* ("thing, property") ← PIE *Hreh-i-* ("wealth, goods")

**Alternative Theory:** Some connect *res* to PIE *reǵ-* "to guide, direct" or *rey-* "count, possession, thing, regulate"

## 3. Semantic Shift

**What Changed:** The word entered English through *legal terminology*. The earliest attested use of "real" was in law: "relating to things" (property) as opposed to "personal" (people). It referred specifically to immovable property—*real estate*.

**When:**
- Early 14c: "Actually existing, having physical existence" (not imaginary)
- Mid-15c: "Relating to things" (especially property)—the legal sense
- 1550s: "Genuine, authentic"
- 1847: "Unaffected, no-nonsense"
- 1960s (U.S. college slang): "Get real" = face facts

**Why:** The legal system needed to distinguish between property rights (*res*) and personal rights. This technical distinction gradually expanded into general usage as "real" = material, substantial, not merely imagined or performed.

**Critical Insight:** The very concept of "the real" as opposed to "the imaginary" or "the artificial" is shaped by property law. To be "real" originally meant to be ownable, transferable, legally substantial. The philosophical distinction between reality and appearance has roots in the legal distinction between property and persons.

## 5. The Liberation Angle

**How This Word Controls:**

"Real" functions as a gatekeeper of legitimacy. What is deemed "real" is taken seriously; what is "unreal" or "imaginary" is dismissed. This distinction serves power by invalidating subjective experience, dreams, visions, and alternative ways of knowing.

The legal origin is crucial: "real" originally distinguished what could be owned. The philosophical concept of "reality" thus carries within it the logic of property and commodification. To ask "what is real?" is already to ask "what can be possessed?"

**How to Reclaim:**

Reclaim "real" by recognizing its constructed nature. Reality is not simply "what exists" but what is *recognized* as existing. The power to define the real is the power to define what matters.

Honor the "unreal"—dreams, fiction, imagination—as having their own form of reality. The dichotomy between real and imaginary is a tool of control, not a natural fact.

---

## 6. Reclaiming Strategy

**Practical Actions:**

1. **Question "Real" Demands:** When someone says "get real" or "be realistic," ask: Whose reality? Reality for whom? Under what conditions? The demand for realism often masks a demand for conformity.

2. **Honor Multiple Realities:** Acknowledge that physical reality, social reality, emotional reality, and imaginative reality are different registers, not a hierarchy with "physical" at the top.

3. **Trace the Legal Roots:** When discussing property or ownership, remember that "real estate" preserves the original meaning. Property law shapes our very concept of what exists.

4. **Reclaim the Unreal:** Defend fiction, fantasy, dreams, and imagination as having value independent of their correspondence to "reality." The unreal has shaped history as much as the real.

5. **Realize = Make Real:** Use "realize" in its original sense—not just "understand" but "bring into existence." To realize something is to make it real through action.
