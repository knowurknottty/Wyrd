# WYRD Entry: ARTIFICIAL
**WYRD Code:** ART-016  
**Confidence Rating:** HIGH (85%)

---

## 1. Surface Reading

**Common Meaning:** Artificial means made or produced by human beings rather than occurring naturally; imitation, simulated; affected, insincere.

**Associations:** Synthetic, fake, manufactured, contrived, imitation, false, man-made, technical.

**Emotional Response:** Artificial evokes suspicion and inferiority compared to "natural." Yet it also evokes admiration for human ingenuity. The word carries the anxiety of authenticity in modern life.

## 2. Root Excavation

**English → Late 14c:** *artificial* — from Old French *artificial*

**Old French → Latin:** *artificialis* — "of or belonging to art"

**Latin → Components:** *artificium* — "a work of art; skill; craft; theory, system"

**Latin *artificium*:** From *artifex* — "craftsman, artist, master of an art"

**Latin *artifex*:** *ars* (art, skill) + *-fex* (maker, from *facere* "to do, make")

**PIE Root:** *ar-* ("to fit together") + *dhe-* ("to set, put, do")

## 3. Semantic Shift

**What Changed:** The original sense was positive: "made with skill, crafted, artistic." The negative sense ("fake, insincere") developed later as "natural" became the valorized term.

**When:**
- Late 14c: "not natural or spontaneous" (but not necessarily negative)
- Early 15c: "made by man, contrived by human skill and labor"
- 1590s: "full of affectation, insincere"
- 1640s: "fictitious, assumed, not genuine"

**Why:** The Romantic movement (18th-19th c.) elevated "nature" over "artifice," authenticity over craft. What was once skilled making became suspicious simulation.

The association with deception comes from the fact that skilled making can imitate nature—artificial flowers, artificial light. The skill itself becomes suspect.

**Critical Insight:** *Artifice* meant both "skill, craft" and "cunning device, trick" (1650s). The same skill that creates beauty can create deception. This ambivalence is built into the concept.

## 5. The Liberation Angle

**How This Word Controls:**

"Artificial" is the devalued half of the natural/artificial binary. By labeling something "artificial," we dismiss it as fake, inferior, suspect. This serves marketing ("natural" products) and ideology (appeals to "authentic" identity).

The etymology reveals that artificial originally meant *skilled making*, *craft*. The devaluation of craft in favor of "nature" serves industrial capitalism by obscuring the skill in all making.

The association with deception (*artifice* as trick) pathologizes human creativity. To make is to potentially deceive; better to "be natural" (i.e., consume "natural" products).

**How to Reclaim:**

Reclaim artificial by returning to its roots: *art* + *making*. Artificial is human skill, craft, creativity. It's not the opposite of authentic but the domain of achieved authenticity.

Honor artifice as human capability. We are the art-making, tech-making, world-transforming animal. This is not shameful but magnificent—when directed toward flourishing.

---

## 6. Reclaiming Strategy

**Practical Actions:**

1. **Celebrate Artifice:** Remember that *artifice* = skill + craft. Artificial intelligence, artificial organs, artificial materials extend human capability. Judge by function and ethics, not "naturalness."

2. **Study *Ars*:** Latin *ars* meant skill in general—not just fine arts but any craft. Reclaim the dignity of making, building, crafting.

3. **Reject Natural/Artificial Binary:** Humans are natural; our works are natural. The question is not "is it natural?" but "does it serve flourishing?"

4. **Artifice as Strategy:** The cunning sense of *artifice* (trick, device) can be reclaimed as strategic thinking. Not deception for harm but cleverness for good.

5. **Make Things:** The ultimate reclamation is making. Cook, build, write, code, craft. Be an *artifex*—a maker.
