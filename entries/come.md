#### Come

**Word:** Come  
**Type:** Verb (Action/Motion)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /kʌm/

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** To move toward; to arrive; to happen; to become
- **Associations:** Coming home, coming true, coming to terms, "come what may"
- **Emotional response:** Anticipation ("it's coming!"), relief ("help has come"), arrival, sexual intimacy

Come is the complementary opposite of go—the motion toward rather than away, the arrival that completes the departure. The etymology reveals: COME comes from REACHING—the purposeful movement toward a destination.

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

**English → Old English:** *cuman*
- Meaning: "to move with the purpose of reaching; to arrive; appear, become perceptible; recover; assemble"
- Past tense: *cuom*, *com* (replaced by came from Old Norse *kvam*)
- From Proto-Germanic *kwem-

**Proto-Germanic → PIE:** *gwa- / *gwem-
- Meaning: "to go, come"
- Also source of "become," "welcome," "income," "outcome"

**Critical Discovery:** COME comes from **REACHING—the directed movement toward a goal, the purposeful arrival.**

**The Go/Come Parity:**
- *Go* = release, departure, letting go (*ghē-*)
- *Come* = reaching, arrival, moving toward (*gwa-*)
- Together they form the basic vocabulary of motion: away and toward

**The Welcome Connection:**
"Welcome" = *wil* (desire, will) + *cuma* (comer, arrival)
- Literally: "one whose coming suits our will"
- To welcome is to have your desire and the arrival align

---

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

| Period | Meaning | Evidence |
|--------|---------|----------|
| PIE | Go, come | *gwa-* |
| Germanic | Arrive, reach | *kwem-* |
| OE | Come, approach, recover | *cuman* |
| 12c. | Happen, occur | "Come to pass" |
| 14c. | Invitation, appeal | "Come, come!" |
| 15c. | Sexual sense | "Lie with" |
| 16c. | Become (with to) | "Come to be" |
| 20c. | Climax | Sexual slang |

**What was added:**
- Existential sense (coming to be)
- Sexual/intimate sense
- Accomplishment sense (coming true)
- Invitation sense (calling someone)

**What was lost:**
- The explicit "reaching" meaning
- The purposeful aspect
- Connection to "become" as arrival at state

**Who benefited:**
- Hospitality industry ("welcome")
- Sexual liberation (reclaiming the word)
- Self-help ("come into your power")
- Those who want arrival to seem effortless

**Step 4: Related Forms**

**From PIE *gwa- "go, come":**

**English derivatives:**
- **Come** - the word itself
- **Become** - to come to be
- **Welcome** - well + come
- **Income** - what comes in
- **Outcome** - what comes out
- **Overcome** - to come over, conquer
- **Shortcoming** - where one falls short
- **Upcoming** - coming up

**Latin cognates (from *gwa-):**
- **Venire** - to come
- **Advent** - coming toward
- **Convention** - coming together
- **Intervene** - come between
- **Inventory** - coming upon, finding
- **Revenue** - coming back

**The Become Connection:**
*Be-* (about, by) + *come* = "to come about, happen, befall"
- To become is to arrive at a state
- Coming into being = becoming

---

**Step 5: The Liberation Angle**

**The Coming as Gift**

To come is to offer oneself as arrival:
- The guest comes
- The opportunity comes
- The revelation comes
- The lover comes

Coming is vulnerable—you expose yourself to the destination, to the welcome or rejection waiting there.

**The Coming vs. Arriving**

We say "come" while in motion, "arrive" when complete. Coming is the process, the approach, the anticipation. To come is to be almost there.

**The Hidden Assumption**
Come assumes:
- There is a destination
- The destination wants you
- Arrival is possible
- Coming completes something

But:
- Some destinations are traps
- Some welcomes are false
- Coming can be invasion
- Not all journeys should complete

---

**Step 6: Reclaiming Strategy**

**1. Honor the Approach**
Coming is not instantaneous—it's approach, gradual arrival. Value the approaching, not just the arrived.

**2. Distinguish Comings**
- **Coming toward** (approach)
- **Coming from** (origin)
- **Coming into** (transformation)
- **Coming to** (recovery, realization)

**3. Practice Welcome**
If coming is arrival, welcome is the reception. Practice receiving what comes—opportunity, guest, truth, pain.

**4. Question Compulsion**
Not everything that comes is welcome. Develop the discernment to say "do not come" when needed.

**5. The Frequency Warrior's Glossary**

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Come here" | "Approach" / "Join me" |
| "Come true" | "Manifest" / "Realize" |
| "Come to terms" | "Reach agreement" |
| "Come what may" | "Whatever arrives" |
| "Coming" (sexual) | Context-specific clarity |

**6. The Final Inversion**

Modernity sees coming as automatic, arrival as entitlement. The etymology reveals coming as **REACHING—purposeful movement toward, the vulnerable act of approach.**

The Frequency Warrior comes with intention:
- Knowing what destination calls
- Offering arrival as gift
- Receiving welcome with grace
- Understanding that to come is to change both self and destination

> "Come to the edge, he said. They said: We are afraid. Come to the edge, he said. They came. He pushed them and they flew." — Guillaume Apollinaire

The Frequency Warrior comes to the edge—and either flies or builds a bridge.
