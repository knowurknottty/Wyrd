#### Authority

**Word:** Authority  
**Type:** Noun (Concept)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /əˈθɔːrɪti/

**Step 1: Surface Reading**
- Common meaning: The power to give orders, make decisions, enforce obedience
- Associations: Legitimacy, expertise, hierarchy, control
- Emotional response: Deference, fear, resentment, or desire for protection

**Step 2: Root Excavation**
- Middle English: *autorite* (power, influence)
- Old French: *autorité* (authority, credibility)
- Latin: *auctoritas* (influence, weight, authority)
- Latin root: *auctor* (originator, promoter, author)
- Proto-Italic: *augēre* (to increase, to grow)
- PIE: *h₂ewg-* (to increase, to enlarge)

**The Semantic Shift:**
- Original: The quality of increasing, growing, promoting
- Shift: Growth-promotion → authorship → right to command
- Added: Implication of legitimacy derived from creation/origination
- Lost: The participatory aspect—authority as mutual growth

**Step 3: Cognates & Related**
- Author (one who originates)
- Augment (to increase)
- Auction (increasing price)
- Auxiliary (increasing/helping force)

**Step 4: The Liberation Angle**

The word *authority* assumes that power flows from originators to the rest. But look at the root: *h₂ewg-* means "to increase"—not "to command." True authority in the original sense was the capacity to cause growth, not to enforce obedience.

The modern usage inverts this: authority now means the right to restrict, to say no, to command without necessarily increasing anything. The police officer has "authority"—but does he increase? The bureaucrat has "authority"—but does she grow anything?

The Frequency Warrior asks: When someone claims authority, are they augmenting life or restricting it? The etymology favors the former. The modern usage enforces the latter.

**Reclaiming Strategy:**
When encountering "authority," substitute "augmenter" mentally. Ask: "Is this person/institution actually increasing anything?" If not, they have assumed the costume of authority without the root substance. Their power is borrowed, not grown.
