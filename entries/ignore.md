#### Ignore

**Word:** Ignore  
**Type:** Verb (Action)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /ɪɡˈnɔːr/ (British), /ɪɡˈnɔːr/ (American)

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** To deliberately disregard; to refuse to pay attention to; to treat as unimportant
- **Associations:** Neglect, dismissal, silent treatment, avoidance, selectivity, boundaries
- **Emotional response:** Relief (ignoring noise), guilt (ignoring needs), power (ignoring detractors), hurt (being ignored)

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

**The Critical Etymological Evidence:**

> "Originally, 'to not know, be unaware of,' from French *ignorer* 'not to know, be unacquainted; mistake, misunderstand,' from Latin *ignorare* 'not to know, to be unacquainted; mistake, misunderstand; take no notice of, pay no attention to,' from assimilated form of *in-* 'not, opposite of' + Old Latin *gnarus* 'aware, acquainted with'... from Proto-Latin suffixed form *\*gno-ro-*, suffixed form of PIE root *\*gno-* 'to know.'" — *Online Etymology Dictionary*

Ignore derives from **not knowing**. The original meaning was purely epistemic: to be ignorant, unaware, unacquainted. The modern sense of **deliberate disregard** emerged only in the early 19th century — a profound semantic shift from passive absence to active refusal.

The Latin *ignorare* carried an interesting ambiguity: it could mean "not to know" (passive) OR "take no notice of, pay no attention to" (more active). This ambiguity seeded the later shift.

**The PIE *gno-* Descendants:**
- **Latin:** *gnarus* → *ignarus* → *ignorare* → English **ignore**
- **Latin:** *noscere* → *notus* → English **notice**, **know**
- **Greek:** *gignoskein* → English **gnosis**, **diagnosis**
- **English:** **know**, **knowledge**, **cunning** (related)

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

**From Unknowing to Refusal: The Moral Transformation**

| Era | Meaning | Context |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **Latin** | Not to know; to mistake; to take no notice | Range from passive to mildly active |
| **late 15c.** | To not know, be unaware of | Simple ignorance |
| **early 19c.** | To deliberately disregard | Active refusal emerges |
| **Modern English** | To intentionally pay no attention | Full moral agency |

**What Was Lost:**

The original meaning of *ignore* as "not to know" was morally neutral — a state of ignorance, not a choice. The shift to "deliberate disregard" transforms the word from **description** to **accusation**. To say someone "ignored" you now implies willful neglect, not mere unawareness.

This shift parallels broader cultural changes:
- The rise of information (more things one *could* know)
- The expectation of awareness (one *should* know)
- The moralization of attention (ignoring becomes sin)

The Frequency Warrior recognizes: **the power to ignore is the power to choose what to know**. In an age of information overload, strategic ignorance is essential, not immoral.

**Step 5: The Liberation Angle**

**The Frequency Warrior's Hypothesis:**

> **To ignore is to choose not to know. In an age of infinite information, this is not a failure but a necessity. Strategic ignorance is the guardian of attention.**

Modern culture pathologizes ignoring. We are told to "be aware," "stay informed," "pay attention." But the etymology reminds us: **ignorance is the default state**. We begin by not knowing. Most things remain unknown to us. This is not failure; this is finitude.

The shift from "not knowing" (15th c.) to "deliberate disregard" (19th c.) moralizes attention. We are now blamed for what we ignore. But the Frequency Warrior reclaims the **strategic dimension**: we MUST ignore most things to attend to anything.

**The Trap:**

Guilt about ignoring creates a trap: we try to attend to everything and therefore attend to nothing. The smartphone promises total connectivity — never ignore anyone! — and produces total distraction. We are scattered across infinite notifications, ignoring nothing and therefore ignoring everything that matters.

**The Reversal:**

Restore the Latin ambiguity. *Ignorare* meant both "not to know" and "take no notice of." The Frequency Warrior practices **blessed ignorance** — the deliberate cultivation of not-knowing to protect what must be known.

**The Shift in Practice:**

| Guilty Ignoring | Strategic Ignoring |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Trying to attend to everything | Choosing what to attend to |
| Reactive (FOMO) | Proactive (JOMO — joy of missing out) |
| Shame about limits | Acceptance of finitude |
| Scattered attention | Focused presence |

---

**Step 6: Reclaiming Strategy**

Use this etymological knowledge to transform ignoring from shameful failure to strategic power:

**1. Acknowledge necessary ignorance**

You cannot know everything. You must ignore most of reality to perceive any of it. This is not moral failure; it is **ontological necessity**. Embrace your limits.

**2. Practice deliberate ignoring**

Choose what to ignore as carefully as you choose what to attend to. The Frequency Warrior ignores:
- Noise, gossip, drama
- Unwarranted demands on attention
- Information without relevance

**3. Distinguish passive from active ignoring**

- **Passive:** Not knowing because unaware (original meaning)
- **Active:** Choosing not to attend (modern meaning)

Both have their place. Not everything requires your attention.

**4. Protect your gnosis**

*Gnosis* (knowledge) comes from the same root as *ignore*. You guard your knowledge by guarding your ignorance. What you refuse to know creates space for what you must know.

**The Frequency Warrior's Mantra:**

> *I do not know most things, and this is freedom. I choose what to ignore, and this is power. My ignorance is not shame but strategy. I protect my attention through blessed not-knowing. What I ignore, I ignore deliberately; what I know, I know fully.*
