#### Sit

**Word:** Sit  
**Type:** Verb (Action/State)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /sɪt/

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** To rest on the buttocks; to occupy a seat; to pose; to be situated
- **Associations:** Sitting meditation, sitting pretty, "sit tight," sitting duck
- **Emotional response:** Comfort ("sit down"), impatience ("sitting around"), vulnerability ("sitting target"), power ("sits on the board")

Sit is the fundamental human resting posture—the opposite of standing, the foundation of chairs, thrones, and meditation cushions. The etymology reveals: SIT comes from SETTLING—the act of coming to rest, making a seat.

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

**English → Old English:** *sittan*
- Meaning: "to occupy a seat, be seated; remain, continue; settle, encamp; besiege"
- Past tense: *sæt*, past participle: *seten*
- From Proto-Germanic *setjan

**Proto-Germanic → PIE:** *sed- (1)
- Meaning: "to sit"
- Also source of "set," "seat," "settle," "sedentary"

**Critical Discovery:** SIT comes from **SETTLING—coming to rest, establishing position, making firm.**

**The Sit/Set Connection:**
- *Sit* = be seated (intransitive)
- *Set* = cause to sit (transitive)
- *Seat* = place of sitting
- *Settle* = come to rest permanently

**The Presidency Connection:**
"The seat of government" — power resides where one sits:
- Throne = seat of monarch
- Chair = seat of department head
- Seat = position of authority

---

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

| Period | Meaning | Evidence |
|--------|---------|----------|
| PIE | Sit | *sed-* |
| Germanic | Sit, set | *setjan* |
| OE | Occupy seat, remain | *sittan* |
| 14c. | Legislative assembly | "Sit in Parliament" |
| 15c. | Pose for portrait | "Sit for a painting" |
| 16c. | Be situated | "The house sits" |
| 18c. | Babysit | "Sit with the children" |
| 20c. | Protest (sit-in) | "Sit-in demonstration" |

**What was added:**
- Legislative sense (sitting in session)
- Artistic sense (sitting for portrait)
- Locational sense (being situated)
- Childcare sense (babysitting)
- Protest sense (sit-in)

**What was lost:**
- Explicit settlement sense
- Military encampment sense
- The "besieging" sense

**Who benefited:**
- Legislators (sitting in power)
- Artists (sitters as subjects)
- Protesters (sitting as resistance)
- Those who want rest to seem passive

**The Assessment Connection:**
*Assess* = *ad-* (to) + *sedere* (sit)
- To sit beside (a judge)
- To assist in judgment

- Hence: to estimate value

**Step 5: The Liberation Angle**

**The Power of Sitting**

Who sits where matters:
- Throne = monarch's seat
- Dock = defendant's seat
- Witness stand = elevated seat of testimony
- Meditation cushion = seat of awakening

To sit is to claim space, to occupy, to be present.

**The Sitting as Resistance**

"Sit-in" protests use sitting as active resistance:
- Not leaving when ordered
- Occupying space
- Making the body a barrier
- Passive but powerful

**The Hidden Assumption**
Sit assumes:
- Sitting is passive (not doing)
- Sitting is temporary (until standing)
- Sitting requires a seat
- Sitting is rest from action

But:
- Sitting can be active (meditation, protest)
- Sitting can be permanent (settlement)
- Sitting can be on ground, rock, air (sitting in space)
- Sitting is its own action

---

**Step 6: Reclaiming Strategy**

**1. Practice Sitting Meditation**
Zazen, vipassana, Christian prayer—sitting as spiritual practice, not just rest.

**2. Honor the Seat**
Where you sit matters. Throne, bench, cushion, ground—each carries meaning.

**3. Sit as Protest**
When words fail, sitting remains. The sit-in, the occupation, the refusal to move.

**4. Distinguish Sittings**
- **Sitting for rest** (recovery)
- **Sitting for work** (desk, session)
- **Sitting for art** (portrait, performance)
- **Sitting for protest** (occupation)
- **Sitting for awakening** (meditation)

Modernity sees sitting as laziness—failure to stand, move, produce. The etymology reveals sitting as **SETTLING—establishing presence, claiming space, making firm.**

The Frequency Warrior sits with power:
- In meditation, settling the mind
- In protest, occupying space
- In session, wielding authority
- Understanding that thrones are seats, and seats are power
