#### Go

**Word:** Go  
**Type:** Verb (Action/Motion)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /ɡoʊ/

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** To move from one place to another; to depart; to function; to become
- **Associations:** Going forward, going away, going crazy, going well, "just go with it"
- **Emotional response:** Excitement ("let's go!"), anxiety ("where do we go?"), resignation ("here we go again")

Go is perhaps the most fundamental verb of motion—the first word a toddler masters, the engine of narrative, the opposite of stay. The etymology reveals: GO comes from RELEASE—the act of letting go, being released, moving away from constraint.

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

**English → Old English:** *gan*
- Meaning: "to advance, walk; depart, go away; happen, take place; conquer; observe, practice"
- Past tense: *eode* (replaced by *went* from *wendan* in 1400s)
- From West Germanic *gaian

**West Germanic → PIE:** *ghē-
- Meaning: "to release, let go; be released"
- Cognates: Sanskrit *jihite* "goes away," Greek *kikhano* "I reach, meet with"

**Critical Discovery:** GO comes from **RELEASE—the act of freeing from constraint, letting go, moving away.**

**The Release/Motion Connection:**
Primitive going:
- To go = to be released from where you are
- Movement as liberation from stasis
- The arrow released from the bow
- The prisoner released from bonds

**The Went Anomaly:**
Modern English "went" (past tense of go) comes from *wendan* "to turn, direct one's way"—completely different verb. Go and went merged into one paradigm, like be/am/was/is.

---

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

| Period | Meaning | Evidence |
|--------|---------|----------|
| PIE | Release, let go | *ghē-* |
| Germanic | Go, walk | *gaian* |
| OE | Advance, depart, happen | *gan* |
| 13c. | Cease to exist | "Go from the world" |
| 14c. | Appear (of dress) | "How does it go?" |
| 15c. | Be sold | "Going for $5" |
| 16c. | Function | "The clock goes" |
| 20c. | Say (teen slang) | "So I go..." |

**What was added:**
- Existential sense (ceasing to exist)
- Commercial sense (being sold)
- Functional sense (working properly)
- Performative sense (quoting speech)

**What was lost:**
- The explicit "release" meaning
- Connection to liberation
- The sense of being set free

**Who benefited:**
- Commerce ("going, going, gone!")
- Technology ("it goes" = it works)
- Those who want motion to seem natural
- Those who want departure to seem easy

---

**Step 4: Related Forms**

**From PIE *ghē- "release, let go":**

**Germanic derivatives:**
- **Go** - the word itself
- **Gone** - departed
- **Going** - departure, progress
- **Gang** - group going together (from *gang* "a going")
- **Forgo** - to go past, abstain
- **Undergo** - to experience (go under)
- **Ago** - gone by (*a-* + *gone*)

**Sanskrit cognates:**
- *Jihite* - goes away
- *Hā-* - leave, abandon

**The Gang Connection:**
"Gang" originally meant "a going, journey"—a group defined by shared movement. Now it carries darker connotations, but the core is still: people who go together.

---

**Step 5: The Liberation Angle**

**The Paradox of Going**

To go is to:
- Leave something behind (loss)
- Move toward something new (hope)
- Release oneself from constraint (freedom)
- Enter the unknown (anxiety)

Going is always double—liberation and departure, adventure and abandonment.

**The "Going" as Identity**

"How's it going?" — we define ourselves by our motion, our trajectory. To be going well is to be well. To be going badly is existential failure.

**The Hidden Assumption**
Go assumes:
- Motion is good ("get going")
- Stasis is bad ("don't just stand there")
- Direction is less important than movement
- Going is simple

But:
- Some going is fleeing
- Some staying is strength
- Direction matters more than speed
- Going requires somewhere to go

---

**Step 6: Reclaiming Strategy**

**1. Honor What You Leave**
Going releases you from where you are—but where you are has value. Don't go without acknowledging what you're releasing.

**2. Distinguish Types of Going**
- **Going toward** (aspiration)
- **Going from** (escape)
- **Going with** (acceptance)
- **Going through** (endurance)
- **Going for** (ambition)

**3. Value the Stay**
The ability to not go—to remain, to stay present, to resist the urge to move—is equally valuable. Not all who wander are lost, but not all who stay are stuck.

**4. Question "Get Up and Go"**
The cult of productivity says "just go." But going without purpose is just running. Ask: where? why? what is released?

**5. The Frequency Warrior's Glossary**

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "How's it going?" | "How are you?" / "What's present?" |
| "Here we go again" | "A pattern repeats" |

| "Way to go" | "Well done" |

**6. The Final Inversion**

Modernity sees going as natural, staying as failure. The etymology reveals going as **RELEASE—liberation from constraint, the act of being set free to move.**

The Frequency Warrior goes with awareness:
- Knowing what is released
- Choosing direction deliberately
- Valuing the staying as much as the going
- Understanding that every going is also an arrival

> "I am not bound to win, but I am bound to be true. I am not bound to succeed, but I am bound to live up to what light I have." — Abraham Lincoln

The Frequency Warrior goes toward the light—not away from darkness, but toward what calls.
