#### Bottom

**Word:** Bottom  
**Type:** Noun (Position)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /ˈbɒtəm/ (British), /ˈbɑːtəm/ (American)

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** Lowest part; foundation; buttocks; essence
- **Associations:** Foundation, base, shame, substrate, fundament
- **Emotional response:** Groundedness, humiliation, stability, depth

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

| Stage | Form | Meaning | Notes |
|-------|------|---------|-------|
| **Modern English** | *bottom* | Lowest part, foundation, buttocks | Multiple senses |
| **Middle English** | *botme* | Ground, soil, lowest part | From Old English |
| **Old English** | *botm* / *bodem* | Ground, soil, foundation | West Germanic |
| **Proto-Germanic** | *buthm-* | Ground, bottom | PIE connection debated |
| **PIE** | *bhudhno-* | Bottom | Possible root |

**The Etymological Evidence:**

> "Middle English botme, from Old English botm, bodan 'ground, soil, foundation, lowest or deepest part of anything,' from Proto-Germanic *buthm- (source also of Old Frisian boden 'soil,' Old Norse botn, Dutch bodem, Old High German bodam, German Boden 'ground, earth, soil'). This is perhaps from PIE root *bhudhno- 'bottom' (source also of Sanskrit budhnah, Avestan buna- 'bottom,' Greek pythmen 'foundation,' Latin fundus 'bottom, piece of land, farm,' Old Irish bond 'sole of the foot')." — *Online Etymology Dictionary*

*Bottom* derives from "ground, soil, foundation" — not merely "lowest point" but *the ground itself*. The bottom is what everything rests upon.

The possible PIE *bhudhno-* connects bottom to:
- **Sanskrit:** *budhnah* = bottom, base
- **Greek:** *pythmen* = foundation
- **Latin:** *fundus* = bottom, foundation, farm
- **Old Irish:** *bond* = sole of foot

**Critical insight:** Bottom is not failed top — it is foundation (*fundus*). The bottom is the ground that supports, the soil that nourishes, the foundation that enables height.

**What Was Lost:**

The original "ground, soil" sense (*botm* as earth) has been narrowed to "lowest point." We've lost the connection between bottom and *fundus* (foundation, farm). The bottom is not just "under" — it is the productive ground.

Latin *fundus* = bottom = foundation = farm. The bottom is where growth begins, not just where descent ends. The English separation of "bottom" (low) from "fund" (foundation) obscures this unity.
