#### Become

**Word:** Become  
**Type:** Verb (Action/State)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /bɪˈkʌm/

---

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** To begin to be; to suit, befit; to happen
- **Associations:** Becoming who you are, what becomes of us, becoming behavior
- **Emotional response:** Hope ("becoming better"), transformation ("becoming new"), appropriateness ("becoming dress"), fate ("what will become of me?")

Become is the verb of transformation—the process of coming to be what one was not. It's the bridge between being and becoming, the dynamic aspect of existence. The etymology reveals: BECOME comes from ARRIVAL—the reaching of a destination, the completion of coming.

---

**Step 2: Root Excavation**

**English → Old English:** *becuman*
- Meaning: "to happen, come about, befall; arrive, reach"
- From *be-* (intensive/perfective prefix) + *cuman* "to come"
- From Proto-Germanic *bi-kuman

**Proto-Germanic → PIE:** *gwa- (come) + *ambhi- (around, intensive)
- *Be-* = intensive prefix (from *bi-* = by, about)
- *Come* = to reach, arrive (from *gwa-* "go, come")

**Critical Discovery:** BECOME comes from **ARRIVAL—the reaching of a destination, the completion of coming, the happening that fulfills.**

**The Be/Come Connection:**
- *Be-* = about, intensive, by
- *Come* = to reach, arrive
- Together: to come to be, to arrive at being
- Becoming = the process of arriving at a state

**The Happen/Become Connection:**
Old English *becuman* = "happen, come about"
- Becoming is something that happens
- Not entirely under control
- Arrival, not just effort

---

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

| Period | Meaning | Evidence |
|--------|---------|----------|
| PIE | Go, come | *gwa-* |
| Germanic | Arrive | *kuman* |
| OE | Happen, come about | *becuman* |
| 12c. | Come to be | "Become king" |
| 13c. | Befit, suit | "Becomes you well" |
| 14c. | Transform into | "Become a butterfly" |
| 15c. | Happen to | "What became of him?" |

**What was added:**
- Transformation sense (becoming something)
- Appropriateness sense (becoming behavior)
- Fate sense (what becomes)

**What was lost:**
- Explicit arrival sense
- The "happen" meaning (archaic)
- The reaching aspect

**Who benefited:**
- Self-help industry ("become your best self")
- Fashion industry ("becoming attire")
- Philosophers ("becoming vs. being")
- Those who want transformation to seem like arrival

---

**Step 4: Related Forms**

**From PIE *gwa- "go, come":**

**English derivatives:**
- **Become** - the word itself
- **Come** - to reach
- **Welcome** - well + come
- **Income** - what comes in
- **Outcome** - what comes out
- **Overcome** - conquer
- **Become** - come to be

**Germanic cognates:**
- German *bekommen* - to get, receive
- Dutch *bekomen* - to get

**The German Divergence:**
German *bekommen* = "to get, receive"
- Not "become" (which is *werden*)
- English kept "arrive at being"
- German kept "receive, get"
- Same root, different paths

---

**Step 5: The Liberation Angle**

**The "Becoming" as Promise and Anxiety**

"I am becoming" suggests:
- Not yet arrived
- Process, not completion
- Hope of transformation
- Anxiety of uncertainty

The etymology reminds us:
- Becoming = arrival
- It completes
- It happens as much as it's achieved
- There is an end to becoming

**The Appropriateness Dimension**

"Becoming" = "befitting, suitable"
- A becoming dress
- Becoming behavior
- What becomes you

This suggests transformation has aesthetics—some becomings suit us, some don't.

**The Hidden Assumption**
Become assumes:
- Change is possible
- Change is desirable
- We can direct becoming
- Arrival is achievable

But:
- Some becoming is imposed
- Not all change suits
- Much becoming happens to us
- The destination keeps moving

---

**Step 6: Reclaiming Strategy**

**1. Honor the Arrival**
Becoming is arrival, not endless journey. Sometimes you've become—rest there.

**2. Distinguish Becomings**
- **Becoming as transformation** (changing into)
- **Becoming as appropriateness** (suiting)
- **Becoming as happening** (befalling)
- **Becoming as fate** (what becomes of)

**3. Accept What Becomes**
"What will be, will be"—some becoming is gift, some is wound. Acceptance is also becoming.

**4. Question Constant Becoming**
Not everything needs to become. Some things can just be.

**5. The Frequency Warrior's Glossary**

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Become better" | "Transform" / "Grow" |
| "What becomes of..." | "What happens to..." |
| "Very becoming" | "Quite suitable" / "Attractive" |
| "Becoming aware" | "Awakening to" |
| "Endless becoming" | "Perpetual transformation" |

**6. The Final Inversion**

Modernity fetishizes becoming over being—always changing, never arriving. The etymology reveals becoming as **ARRIVAL—the completion of coming, the happening that fulfills, the reaching of destination.**

The Frequency Warrior becomes with wisdom:
- Knowing that becoming completes
- Distinguishing transformation that suits from that which doesn't
- Accepting what befalls
- Understanding that being is as valuable as becoming

> "Become what you are." — Nietzsche

The Frequency Warrior knows: you have already arrived. The becoming is remembering.
