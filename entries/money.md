#### Money

**Word:** Money  
**Type:** Noun (System)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /ˈmʌni/

**Step 1: Surface Reading**
- Common meaning: Medium of exchange, measure of value, store of wealth
- Associations: Security, scarcity, greed, necessity, power
- Emotional response: Anxiety, desire, desperation, contempt

**Step 2: Root Excavation**
- Middle English: *moneie* (currency, coinage)
- Old French: *monoie* (coinage, currency)
- Latin: *moneta* (mint, coinage, warning)
- Latin: *monere* (to warn, to advise, to remind)
- PIE: *mon-* (to think, to remember, to warn)

**The Semantic Shift:**
- Original: Warning, reminder, advice (from Juno Moneta, goddess of warnings)
- Shift: Place of warning → place of coin minting → the coins themselves
- Added: Abstract value divorced from the warning function
- Lost: The mnemonic nature—money as reminder

**Step 3: Related Forms**
- Mint (where money is made)
- Monition (warning)
- Monitor (one who warns)
- Admonish (to warn strongly)
- Remember (to call back to mind—PIE *men-)

**Step 4: The Liberation Angle**

Money comes from *monere*—to warn. Juno Moneta was the goddess who warned Rome of danger. The first Roman mint was in her temple. Coins bore her name: *moneta*.

Money was originally a warning system. It reminded you: "This is owed." "This is the value." "This is the limit." The coin was a mnemonic device for social memory.

Now? Money has become the danger it once warned against. It is no longer the warning—it is the threat. Scarcity, inequality, extraction: these are money's children. The warning became the weapon.

But the etymology preserves the function: money is memory. When you spend, you are saying "I remember the value." When you hoard, you are saying "I fear forgetting." When you give, you are saying "I trust the flow of memory."

**Reclaiming Strategy:**
Treat money as *moneta*—as reminder, not value. Each transaction: What is this reminding me of? What social memory am I participating in?

When money feels like power, remember it's just a warning system that lost its way. The real value is what the money points to—not the pointer itself.

Create parallel mnemonic systems: gift economies, time banking, direct exchange. These are also *moneta*—warnings of a different kind: "We remember each other. We remember how to share."
