## Entry Summaries

### 141. POVERTY
**File:** `poverty.md` | **WYRD Code:** POVT-001
- **Root:** Latin *paupertas* ← *pauper* (poor) ← PIE *pehw-* (few)
- **Core Meaning:** Having little; originally quantitative, now loaded with moral judgment
- **Liberation Angle:** Poverty is measurement, not destiny. "Having few" is circumstance, not identity.
- **Related:** pauper, poor, paucity

### 142. ABUNDANCE
**File:** `abundance.md` | **WYRD Code:** ABUN-001
- **Root:** Latin *abundantia* ← *abundare* (overflow) ← *unda* (wave) ← PIE *wed-* (water)
- **Core Meaning:** Wave-like overflow; fullness that exceeds container
- **Liberation Angle:** Abundance is flow, not accumulation. Water overflows; it doesn't hoard.
- **Related:** abound, undulate, redundant, water

### 143. SCARCITY
**File:** `scarcity.md` | **WYRD Code:** SCAR-001
- **Root:** Latin *excerpere* (pluck out) ← *carpere* (pluck, harvest) ← PIE *kerp-* (gather)
- **Core Meaning:** Selection's shadow; what remains after picking
- **Liberation Angle:** Scarcity is produced by selection, not natural law. "Not enough" often means "not distributed."
- **Related:** excerpt, harvest, carp (to pluck)

### 144. ENOUGH
**File:** `enough.md` | **WYRD Code:** ENOU-001
- **Root:** Old English *genōg* ← Proto-Germanic *ganōgaz* (collectively reached) ← PIE *nek-* (reach, attain)
- **Core Meaning:** Together-attained sufficiency
- **Liberation Angle:** Enough is relationship, not quantity. Original sense was collective, not individual.
- **Related:** attain, obtain, Sanskrit *asnoti* (reach)

### 145. EXCESS
**File:** `excess.md` | **WYRD Code:** EXCS-001
- **Root:** Latin *excessus* ← *excedere* (go out, depart) ← *ex-* + *cedere* (to go)
- **Core Meaning:** Going beyond; boundary-crossing; departure
- **Liberation Angle:** Excess is transgressive movement, not static accumulation. Some excess is transcendence.
- **Related:** exceed, proceed, succeed, recede, cede

### 146. LACK
**File:** `lack.md` | **WYRD Code:** LACK-001
- **Root:** Middle Dutch *lak* ← PIE *leg-* (2) (dribble, trickle) — root of "leak"
- **Core Meaning:** Leakage; something escaping, not absent
- **Liberation Angle:** Lack is not emptiness but leakage. What leaks can be contained.
- **Related:** leak, lacking, lackluster

### 147. PLENTY
**File:** `plenty.md` | **WYRD Code:** PLEN-001
- **Root:** Latin *plenitas* (fullness) ← *plenus* (full) ← PIE *pele-* (to fill)
- **Core Meaning:** Completeness; filled to capacity
- **Liberation Angle:** Plenty is fullness, not surplus. A full container has edges; surplus is edgeless.
- **Related:** full, fill, complete, deplete, replete

### 148. GROW
**File:** `grow.md` | **WYRD Code:** GROW-001
- **Root:** Old English *growan* ← Proto-Germanic *grō-* ← PIE *ghre-* (to grow, become green)
- **Core Meaning:** Becoming green; organic development
- **Liberation Angle:** Growth is becoming-green — entering living cycles, not exponential expansion.
- **Related:** green, grass, grew, grown

### 149. SHRINK
**File:** `shrink.md` | **WYRD Code:** SHRI-001
- **Root:** Old English *scrincan* (draw in, shrivel) ← Proto-Germanic *skrink-* ← PIE *sker-* (turn, bend)
- **Core Meaning:** Spontaneous protective contraction
- **Liberation Angle:** Shrinking is protective wisdom, not failure. The snail withdraws to survive.
- **Related:** shrivel, shrunk, shrinkage, "shrinking violet"

### 150. EXPAND
**File:** `expand.md` | **WYRD Code:** EXPD-001
- **Root:** Latin *expandere* (spread out) ← *ex-* + *pandere* (spread) ← PIE *pete-* (spread)
- **Core Meaning:** Spreading out; unfolding what was folded
- **Liberation Angle:** Expansion is unfolding potential, not inflation. True expansion reveals; false inflates.
- **Related:** expanse, expansion, expansive, spawn

### 151. CONTRACT
**File:** `contract.md` | **WYRD Code:** CONT-001
- **Root:** Latin *contractus* ← *contrahere* (draw together) ← *con-* + *trahere* (draw, pull)
- **Core Meaning:** Drawing together; binding by pulling into proximity
- **Liberation Angle:** Contraction is binding power — muscles, agreements, focus. Drawing together creates strength.
- **Related:** tractor, tract, trace, train, treat

### 152. INCLUDE
**File:** `include.md` | **WYRD Code:** INCL-001
- **Root:** Latin *includere* (shut in) ← *in-* + *claudere* (shut, close) ← PIE *klau-* (hook, peg)
- **Core Meaning:** Shutting in; enclosure
- **Liberation Angle:** Inclusion requires boundaries — shutting in creates inside. No gate, no inside.
- **Related:** close, enclose, conclude, preclude, seclude, claustrophobia

### 153. EXCLUDE
**File:** `exclude.md` | **WYRD Code:** EXCL-001
- **Root:** Latin *excludere* (shut out) ← *ex-* + *claudere* (shut, close)
- **Core Meaning:** Active shutting out; barring from entry
- **Liberation Angle:** Exclusion creates the outside that makes inside possible. Healthy boundaries require conscious exclusion.
- **Related:** exclusive, exclusion, close, include (opposite)

### 154. BELONG
**File:** `belong.md` | **WYRD Code:** BELN-001
- **Root:** Middle English *belongen* ← *be-* (intensive) + *longen* (go along with) ← Old English *langian*
- **Core Meaning:** Going along with; accompaniment
- **Liberation Angle:** Belonging is practice, not possession. You don't "have" belonging; you "do" belonging.
- **Related:** long (yearn), longing, belongings

### 155. ALIEN
**File:** `alien.md` | **WYRD Code:** ALIE-001
- **Root:** Latin *alienus* ← *alius* (another, other) ← PIE *al-* (beyond)
- **Core Meaning:** Belonging to another; other among many
- **Liberation Angle:** Alien is not enemy but *alius* — another among others. Difference is natural multiplicity.
- **Related:** alias, alibi, alienate, inalienable

### 156. NATIVE
**File:** `native.md` | **WYRD Code:** NATI-001
- **Root:** Latin *nativus* ← *natus* (born) ← *nasci* (be born) ← PIE *gene-* (give birth)
- **Core Meaning:** Produced by birth; innate
- **Liberation Angle:** Nativity is continuous — you are born anew each moment. Birth is process, not single event.
- **Related:** nation, nature, neonatal, innate, pregnant, renaissance

### 157. FOREIGN
**File:** `foreign.md` | **WYRD Code:** FORG-001
- **Root:** Latin *foris* (outside, out of doors) ← PIE *dhwer-* (door)
- **Core Meaning:** Out of doors; beyond the threshold
- **Liberation Angle:** Foreign is out-of-doors — where life happens. Staying inside is stagnation; stepping out is growth.
- **Related:** door, forum, forest, forfeit, hors d'oeuvre

### 158. HERE
**File:** `here.md` | **WYRD Code:** HERE-001
- **Root:** Old English *her* ← Proto-Germanic *hi-* (this) + *-r* ← PIE *ki-* (this, here)
- **Core Meaning:** This place; presence
- **Liberation Angle:** Here is *this*-place, not any place. Presence is demonstrative: attention focused on now.
- **Related:** he, she, it, hence, hither, today (German *heute*)

### 159. THERE
**File:** `there.md` | **WYRD Code:** THER-001
- **Root:** Old English *þær* ← Proto-Germanic *thær* ← PIE *to-* (that, there) + *-r*
- **Core Meaning:** That place; the distal presence
- **Liberation Angle:** There is destination — the elsewhere that gives here direction. Here is root; there is horizon.
- **Related:** that, the, then, than, thus, thence, thither

### 160. NEAR
**File:** `near.md` | **WYRD Code:** NEAR-001
- **Root:** Old English *near* (comparative of *neah* "nigh") ← Proto-Germanic *naehwa-*
- **Core Meaning:** Nearer; close in relation
- **Liberation Angle:** Near is comparative — always in movement toward. Proximity is practice, not possession.
- **Related:** nigh, next (superlative), nearby, nearly, neighbor

## Thematic Connections

### Quantity Words (141-151)
These words trace how humans conceptualize resources and change:
- **Measurement:** poverty (few-ness), plenty (full-ness), enough (sufficiency)
- **Flow:** abundance (overflow), lack (leakage), scarcity (selection's shadow)
- **Movement:** grow (becoming-green), shrink (protective contraction), expand (unfolding), contract (drawing together)
- **Excess** bridges both categories — quantity exceeding boundaries

### Location Words (152-160)
These words map spatial and social boundaries:
- **Enclosure:** include (shut in), exclude (shut out)
- **Belonging:** belong (go along with), alien (other), native (born), foreign (out of doors)
- **Position:** here (this place), there (that place), near (close relation)

### Liberation Themes Across Batch
1. **Process vs. State:** Most words reveal dynamic processes disguised as static states
2. **Relationship vs. Possession:** Enough, belong, near emphasize relationship over having
3. **Boundary Wisdom:** Include/exclude/foreign reveal boundaries as necessary, not oppressive
4. **Movement:** Grow/shrink/expand/contract are cycles, not linear progress
