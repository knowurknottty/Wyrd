# WYRD Entry: PRIVATE
**WYRD Code:** PRI-018  
**Confidence Rating:** HIGH (85%)

## 1. Surface Reading

**Common Meaning:** Private means belonging to or for the use of one particular person or group; not public; personal, confidential; not holding public office.

**Associations:** Personal, secret, intimate, individual, exclusive, non-state, property, autonomy.

**Emotional Response:** Private evokes security, autonomy, and intimacy. Modern liberalism treats privacy as a fundamental right. Yet privacy also enables secrecy, exclusion, and hoarding. The word carries the tension between freedom and isolation.

## 2. Root Excavation

**English → Late 14c:** *private* — "pertaining or belonging to oneself"

**Middle English → Latin:** *privatus* — "set apart (from what is public), belonging to oneself (not to the state), peculiar, personal"

**Latin → Verb:** *privare* — "to bereave, deprive, rob, strip of anything; to free, release, deliver from anything"

**Latin → Adjective:** *privus* — "one's own, individual, peculiar to one"

**Proto-Italic:** *prei-wo-* — "separate, individual"

**PIE Root:** *per-* (1) — "forward" → *prai-*, *prei-* "in front of, before" → "separate"

**Chain:** PRIVATE ← Latin *privatus* ← *privare* ← *privus* ← Proto-Italic *prei-wo-* ← PIE *per-* ("forward, before, separate")

**Semantic Shift within Latin:** From "being in front" (PIE) → "being separate" (Proto-Italic) → "one's own" (Latin)

---

## 3. Semantic Shift

**What Changed:** The original PIE sense was "being in front" (*per-* forward). The shift to "being separate" then "one's own" suggests a development: what is in front is separated from the mass, becomes individual.

**When:**
- Late 14c: "pertaining to oneself, not shared"
- Early 15c: "not holding public office"
- 1550s: "confidential, meant to be secret"
- 1580s: "in private" (privily)
- 1680s: *private property*
- 1797: *private enterprise*

**Why:** The rise of individualism and capitalism transformed private from "deprived of public status" to "privileged individual sphere." Private property became the foundation of liberal political economy.

**Critical Insight:** The Latin verb *privare* means both "to deprive" and "to free, release." Private is simultaneously loss (of public belonging) and gain (of individual autonomy). This ambivalence continues: privacy is both freedom and isolation.

## 5. The Liberation Angle

**How This Word Controls:**

"Private" is the foundational concept of liberal capitalism. Private property, private enterprise, private life—these are presented as natural rights when they are historical constructions serving particular interests.

The association of "private" with "deprivation" (*privare*) reveals the cost: privatization deprives the public of common goods. What is made private is taken from shared access.

Privacy rights protect the powerful (to hide wealth, evade scrutiny) more than the powerless. The private sphere can be a refuge or a prison—domestic violence, exploitation, and abuse often hide behind "privacy."

**How to Reclaim:**

Reclaim private by recognizing its costs and limits. Privacy as autonomy is valuable; privacy as secrecy for power is not. The question is always: private for whom? From whom?

Honor privacy as sanctuary—protection from surveillance and intrusion. But resist privacy as exclusion—hoarding resources, hiding crimes, evading accountability.

---

## 6. Reclaiming Strategy

**Practical Actions:**

1. **Remember *Privare*:** Private = deprived. What is gained by privacy? What is lost? When something is privatized, what is taken from the commons?

2. **Distinguish Privacy from Secrecy:** Privacy protects personhood; secrecy protects power. Defend the first; expose the second.

3. **Question Private Property:** The concept emerged historically; it's not natural. What should be private? What should be common? The answers vary by context.

4. **Protect Intimate Privacy:** The original sense—*belonging to oneself*—protects intimate life, personal relationships, bodily autonomy. Defend this against surveillance capitalism.

5. **Resist Commodification:** "Private" is increasingly marketized (private data, private experience). Reclaim private as personal, not for sale.
