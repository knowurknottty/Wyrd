#### Sun

**Word:** Sun  
**Type:** Noun (Celestial Body/Deity)  
**Language:** English  
**Pronunciation:** /sʌn/

**Step 1: Surface Reading**

- **Common meaning:** The star around which Earth orbits; source of light and heat; center of solar system

**The Chain of Descent:**

**The Critical Etymological Evidence:**

> "Middle English *sonne*, from Old English *sunne* 'the sun,' from Proto-Germanic *\*sunno*... from PIE *\*s(u)wen-*, an alternative form of root *\*sawel-* 'the sun.' Old English *sunne* was fem. (as generally in Germanic), and the fem. pronoun was used in English until 16c.; since then masc. has prevailed, 'without necessarily implying personification.'" — *Online Etymology Dictionary*

The Old English sun was **grammatically feminine** — "she" rather than "he." The shift to masculine pronouns in the 16th century reflects cultural changes, not astronomical ones. Many Germanic languages retain the feminine (German *die Sonne*), while Romance languages use masculine (French *le soleil*, from Latin *sol*).

The PIE root *sawel-* / *s(u)wen-* is the source of sun words across Indo-European:
- **Latin:** *sol* → **solar**, **solstice**
- **Greek:** *helios* → **heliocentric**, **helium**
- **Sanskrit:** *surya* → **the sun deity**

**The PIE *sawel-* / *s(u)wen-* Descendants:**
- **Latin:** *sol* "sun" → **solar**, **solstice**, **parasol**
- **Greek:** *helios* "sun" → **heliotrope**, **helium**, **aphelion**
- **Sanskrit:** *suryaḥ* "sun" → Hindu sun god
- **Old Irish:** *súil* "eye" (the sun as eye of heaven)

**Step 3: Semantic Shift**

**From Goddess to Star: The Desacralization of Sun**

| Era | Meaning | Context |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **PIE (4000+ BCE)** | The sun as deity/life-giver | Religious veneration |
| **Proto-Germanic** | *sunno* — feminine solar divinity | Grammatical gender = conceptual gender |
| **Old English** | *sunne* — feminine, divine, life-giving | "She" shines, warms, enables growth |
| **16th century** | Shift to masculine pronouns | Cultural/religious changes |
| **Modern English** | The sun as astronomical body | Scientific desacralization |

**What Was Lost:**

The grammatical femininity of Old English *sunne* suggests an **archaic solar goddess tradition**, suppressed or forgotten but preserved in grammar. Across Germanic cultures, the sun was "she" — a nurturing, life-giving feminine power.

The shift to masculine "he" in the 16th century parallels:
- The rise of Protestantism (God the Father emphasized)
- The Scientific Revolution (astronomical rather than divine)
- Patriarchal consolidation

Modern English "it" (the sun) completes the **triple alienation**: from goddess to god to object.
