# WYRD Entry: NATURAL
**WYRD Code:** NAT-015  
**Confidence Rating:** HIGH (88%)

## 1. Surface Reading

**Common Meaning:** Natural means existing in or derived from nature; not made or caused by humankind; in accordance with nature; normal, expected, or innate.

**Associations:** Organic, wild, innate, normal, authentic, unspoiled, biological, instinctive.

**Emotional Response:** Natural evokes positive associations—authenticity, health, purity. The "natural" is preferred over the "artificial" in marketing, ethics, and aesthetics. Yet "natural" is one of the most contested concepts.

## 3. Semantic Shift

**What Changed:** The original sense was "by birth, innate, hereditary"—not "from the physical world" but "from one's nature/character." The opposition to "artificial" developed later.

**When:**
- Early 14c: "of one's inborn character; hereditary, innate, by birth"
- 1580s: "in accordance with nature"
- 1660s: "material world beyond human civilization" ("state of nature")

**Why:** The shift reflects changing understandings of what constitutes "nature." When nature was seen as created/divinely ordered, "natural" meant conforming to that order. When nature became "the wild" opposite to civilization, "natural" meant uncivilized.

**Critical Insight:** The PIE root *gene-* gives us both "natural" (*nasci* branch) and "genetic" (*gene* branch). Modern "natural" and "genetic" share ancient roots. The appeal to "nature" is always an appeal to origin, birth, how things fundamentally are.

## 5. The Liberation Angle

**How This Word Controls:**

"Natural" is the primary legitimation strategy in ethics, marketing, and politics. "It's natural" is supposed to end debate. But "natural" is constructed, contested, and historically variable.

The appeal to nature has justified slavery ("natural hierarchy"), patriarchy ("natural roles"), and exploitation ("natural resources"). The "natural" is not innocent.

The opposition between natural and artificial obscures that humans are natural and everything we make is made from natural materials by natural beings. The dichotomy serves marketing and ideology, not understanding.

**How to Reclaim:**

Reclaim natural by remembering its root: *born, innate, from birth*. Natural doesn't mean "untouched by humans" but "according to one's fundamental character."

Reject the natural/artificial binary. Humans are natural; our works are natural; our consciousness is natural. The question is not "is it natural?" but "what kind of natural?"—sustainable or destructive, nurturing or exploitative.

---

## 6. Reclaiming Strategy

**Practical Actions:**

1. **Ask "Natural For Whom?":** When someone invokes "natural," ask: natural for what species? In what environment? At what life stage? Universal "nature" doesn't exist.

2. **Reject Naturalistic Fallacy:** "Is" doesn't imply "ought." Even if something were "natural," that wouldn't make it good. Disease is natural; medicine is "artificial"—which do you prefer?

3. **Study *Physis*:** Greek *physis* (nature) meant "that which grows, that which emerges." Nature is process, not static state. What grows? What emerges?

4. **Honor Innate Nature:** Return to the original sense: "by birth, innate." What is your innate nature? What emerged in you from birth? This is deeper than "wilderness."

5. **Nature Includes Culture:** Remember that *nasci* (to be born) is the root of both "natural" and "nation." We are born into cultures, languages, histories. This is as "natural" as being born into ecosystems.
