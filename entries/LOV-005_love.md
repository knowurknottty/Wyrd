# WYRD Entry: LOVE
**WYRD Code:** LOV-005  
**Confidence Rating:** HIGH (85%)

---

## 1. Surface Reading

**Common Meaning:** Love is a strong feeling of deep affection, attachment, or devotion. It spans romantic attraction, familial bonds, friendship, and spiritual connection. To love is to care deeply, to prioritize another's wellbeing, to desire union.

**Associations:** Romance, commitment, passion, tenderness, sacrifice, union, intimacy, joy, pain.

**Emotional Response:** Love evokes the full spectrum of human emotion—ecstasy and agony, security and vulnerability. It's simultaneously the most celebrated and most feared force in human experience. The word carries nearly sacred weight across cultures.

## 3. Semantic Shift

**What Changed:** The original sense was broader than modern romantic love—it included pleasure, desire, and care in general. The narrowing to romantic/sexual love occurred gradually.

**When:**
- Old English: "feeling of love" broadly
- c. 1200: "beloved person"
- 1742: "no score" in tennis (from "playing for love"—for no stakes)
- c. 1950: "make love" as euphemism for sexual intercourse (earlier meant "pay amorous attention")

**Why:** The semantic narrowing reflects cultural changes in how affection was categorized. Medieval courtly love played a major role in shaping modern romantic ideals. The Puritan era suppressed sexual expression, leading to euphemistic uses.

**Critical Insight:** The PIE root *leubh-* connects love to *libido* and *pleasure*. Love and desire share deep etymological roots—something various moral systems have tried to separate.

## 5. The Liberation Angle

**How This Word Controls:**

"Love" is perhaps the most powerful control mechanism in human society. Demands to "love" one's country, family, or god enforce conformity. The equation of love with possession ("you belong to me") justifies control and violence.

The narrowing of love to romantic/sexual pair-bonding serves capitalist and patriarchal structures by channeling human connection into nuclear family units that consume and reproduce efficiently.

The "love" of abstract concepts (freedom, country, race) mobilizes people for war and exploitation. When love is weaponized, it becomes hate's most effective disguise.

**How to Reclaim:**

Reclaim love by returning to its broader PIE sense: *care, desire, pleasure*. Love need not be possessive to be real. The Buddhist concept of *metta* (loving-kindness without attachment) offers a model.

Separate love from ownership. Love as care for others' flourishing, not control over their choices. Love as pleasure in existence, not desperate clinging.

---

## 6. Reclaiming Strategy

**Practical Actions:**

1. **Expand the Category:** Practice loving beyond romantic pair-bonding—love friends, strangers, places, activities, the world. Reclaim the Old English breadth.

2. **Separate Love from Ownership:** Notice when "I love you" means "I own you" or "you owe me." Reject possessive love. Practice what psychologist David Richo calls "5 A's": Attention, Acceptance, Appreciation, Affection, Allowing.

3. **Study Multiple Loves:** Learn from Greek (*eros, philia, agape*), Sanskrit (*kama, prema, bhakti*), and other languages that distinguish types of love. English's single word is impoverishment.

4. **Reclaim "Playing for Love":** The tennis term reminds us that love can be given freely, without stakes or exchange. Practice love without expectation of return.

5. **Love as Political Act:** In a world structured by competition and fear, choosing to love—care, desire, take pleasure—is radical resistance.
