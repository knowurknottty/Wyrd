## Key Themes

### 1. The Inversion of Meaning

Several words underwent dramatic semantic inversions:
- **Angry**: From suffering constriction → inflicting rage
- **Brave**: From savage/wild → noble courage
- **Coward**: From physical flight → moral deficiency
- **Wealth**: From holistic well-being → mere money

### 2. The Body as Metaphor

Many words originated in embodied experience:
- **Courage** = heart (*cor*)
- **Anger** = throat constriction (*angh-*)
- **Afraid** = removal from peace (embodied security)
- **Calm** = heat → stillness (meteorological → psychological)
- **Hard** = shell, armor (*kar-*)
- **Soft** = fitting together (*sem-*)

### 3. The Social Construction of Virtue

Class and power shaped moral vocabulary:
- **Gentle** = aristocratic manners democratized
- **Rich** = kingly power → financial accumulation
- **Strong** = tight/tense → powerful
- **Brave** = wildness rehabilitated through chivalry

### 4. The Medicalization of Emotion

Several words moved from disease-states to emotions:
- **Rage** = rabies (foaming madness)
- **Anger** = constriction/anguish
- **Fear** = danger/peril (external) → anxiety (internal)

### 5. The PIE Root Network

Major root families represented:
- **\*kerd-** (heart): courage, cardiac, core
- **\*angh-** (narrow): angry, anguish, angina, angst
- **\*sem-** (one/together): soft, same, seem, social
- **\*strenk-** (tight): strong, string, strict, strain
- **\*weik-** (bend): weak, week, wicker, vicissitude
- **\*kar-** (hard): hard, cancer, carbuncle
- **\*reg-** (straight/rule): rich, rex, right, regal

## Liberation Insights

### Fear → Awareness
Fear is not failed courage; it is **intelligence about danger**. Reclaim the external sense: fear points to real threats, not just internal anxiety.

### Courage → Heart-Action
Courage is not fearlessness; it is **acting from the heart regardless of fear**. The etymology connects courage to *any* heart-disposition; choose yours consciously.

### Anger → Constriction
Anger is not aggression; it is **the memory of being squeezed**. Address the constriction, not the target. Release, don't transmit.

### Calm → Siesta
Calm is not escape from heat; it is **stillness within heat**. Don't avoid intensity; develop the capacity to rest within it.

### Strong/Weak → Tension/Bending
Strong = tightly focused (*strang*); weak = yielding (*wāc*). Both are strategies. The oak resists; the willow bends. Each has its season.

### Wealth → Well-Being
Wealth originally meant *wela*—well-being, not money. Reclaim the holistic sense: health, relationships, purpose, freedom, and sufficient material security.
